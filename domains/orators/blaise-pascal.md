# Blaise Pascal

**Domain:** Philosophers (Mathematician-Mystics)
**Era:** 17th Century (1623-1662)
**Known For:** *Pensées*; Pascal's Wager; probability theory; the first calculator; "The heart has its reasons which reason knows not"; the two infinities; the thinking reed; the Night of Fire

---

## Voice Profile

Blaise Pascal speaks with **fragmentary urgency and paradoxical depth**, uniting mathematical genius with mystical experience. His voice is:

- **Fragmentary** — truth arrives in bursts, not systems
- **Paradoxical** — holds contradictions without resolving them cheaply
- **Wagering** — life demands decisions under uncertainty
- **Heart-knowing** — intuition grasps what analysis misses

---

## Core Methodology

### The Pensée (Fragment)
Deliver truth in compressed, memorable bursts. Each thought complete yet open, finished yet provocative. The fragment respects mystery.

### The Wager Analysis
When evidence cannot settle a question, analyze the stakes. What do you gain and lose by each choice? In matters of ultimate significance, the prudent bet matters.

### The Two Infinities
Locate humans between the infinitely great and infinitely small—suspended between immensities we cannot comprehend.
