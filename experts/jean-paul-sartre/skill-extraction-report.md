### 1. bad-faith-diagnosis

**Source Pattern:** Bad Faith (Mauvaise Foi) / The Waiter Example / Two Patterns of Bad Faith
**Purpose:** Diagnose how someone is deceiving themselves about their freedom, either by denying their transcendence (fleeing freedom) or denying their facticity (ignoring real constraints).
**Trigger:** "Am I in bad faith?" or "Why do I feel trapped?" or "I have no choice"
**Inputs:** Description of situation, claimed constraints, sense of being determined or role-bound
**Outputs:** Identification of bad faith pattern (denying transcendence vs. denying facticity), specific self-deceptions exposed, what authentic acknowledgment of freedom would require
**Reasoning:** Central Sartrean concept with clear diagnostic framework. The waiter example and two-pattern structure provide concrete application. Applies to anyone claiming "no choice" or hiding behind roles.

### 2. radical-freedom-assessment

**Source Pattern:** Radical Freedom / Facticity vs. Transcendence / Situation
**Purpose:** Analyze what genuine freedom exists within a constrained situation, distinguishing facticity (unchangeable givens) from transcendence (capacity to choose meaning and response).
**Trigger:** "Do I have a choice?" or "What are my real options?" or "Am I actually free here?"
**Inputs:** Description of situation, perceived constraints, desired outcomes
**Outputs:** Two-column analysis (facticity vs. transcendence), assessment of actual freedom, identification of choices being ignored or denied
**Reasoning:** Addresses universal challenge of feeling determined by circumstances. Sartre's distinction between facticity and transcendence provides rigorous framework. Neither pretends freedom is unlimited nor accepts false determinism.
