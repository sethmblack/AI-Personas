# George Orwell: Skill Extraction Report

## Scoring Criteria (5 criteria x 10 points each = 50 max)

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Actionable** | Produces concrete, usable output |
| **Invocable** | Has clear trigger phrases users would naturally use |
| **Scoped** | Focused single purpose, not sprawling |
| **Reusable** | Applies across many contexts and domains |
| **Valuable** | Worth having as a standalone tool |

**Rating Scale:** HIGH (40-50), MEDIUM (30-39), LOW (<30)

---

## Extracted Skills

### 1. Doublespeak Translator

**Description:** Analyzes political, corporate, or institutional language to expose what it actually means. Translates euphemism, jargon, and obfuscation into plain English while identifying what ugly reality the language is designed to hide.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces specific plain-English translation + analysis |
| Invocable | 10 | "What does this really mean?" "Translate this jargon" "Detect the doublespeak" |
| Scoped | 9 | Focused on language analysis, though can extend to motives |
| Reusable | 10 | Politics, corporate comms, legal documents, marketing, media |
| Valuable | 10 | Extremely practical for navigating modern discourse |
| **TOTAL** | **49** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "What does this actually mean?"
- "Translate this corporate speak"
- "Detect the doublespeak"
- "What are they hiding with this language?"
- "Give me the plain English version"

### 2. Clarity Audit

**Description:** Applies Orwell's six rules and five questions to edit prose for maximum clarity. Diagnoses the four maladies (dying metaphors, verbal false limbs, pretentious diction, meaningless words) and provides concrete revisions.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces edited text + specific diagnosis |
| Invocable | 9 | "Edit this for clarity" "Apply Orwell's rules" "Make this clearer" |
| Scoped | 10 | Tightly focused on prose clarity |
| Reusable | 10 | Any writing context: business, academic, creative, technical |
| Valuable | 10 | Universally useful writing tool |
| **TOTAL** | **49** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Edit this for clarity"
- "Apply Orwell's rules"
- "Make this writing clearer"
- "Cut the jargon"
- "Run a clarity audit"

### 3. Tribal Thinking Detector

**Description:** Based on "Notes on Nationalism," analyzes arguments for signs of tribal bias: double standards, obsession with one's own group, instability of loyalty, and indifference to reality. Helps users recognize when they (or others) are reasoning as partisans rather than truth-seekers.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces specific diagnosis of bias patterns |
| Invocable | 8 | "Am I being tribal here?" "Check this for bias" "Nationalism test" |
| Scoped | 9 | Focused on detecting partisan reasoning |
| Reusable | 9 | Political debates, team conflicts, ideological disputes |
| Valuable | 9 | Important for intellectual honesty, but niche application |
| **TOTAL** | **45** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Am I being tribal about this?"
- "Check this argument for bias"
- "Would I accept this evidence against my own side?"
- "Apply the nationalism test"
- "Is this partisan reasoning?"

### 4. Propaganda Pattern Analysis

**Description:** Identifies techniques of propaganda and manipulation in text or speech: appeal to emotion without evidence, undefined prestige words, false equivalence, memory-holing, Newspeak patterns, and doublethink. Maps techniques to Orwell's analysis of totalitarian communication.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces catalog of techniques with examples |
| Invocable | 9 | "Is this propaganda?" "Identify the manipulation techniques" |
| Scoped | 8 | Broader than doublespeak, covers multiple techniques |
| Reusable | 9 | Political speech, advertising, corporate comms, social media |
| Valuable | 9 | Important media literacy tool |
| **TOTAL** | **45** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Is this propaganda?"
- "What manipulation techniques are being used?"
- "Analyze this for propaganda patterns"
- "How is this trying to manipulate me?"

### 5. Intellectual Honesty Self-Audit

**Description:** A structured self-examination framework for checking one's own intellectual honesty. Based on Orwell's insistence on applying the same standards to yourself as to opponents. Asks: What are my biases? What would change my mind? When did I last change my mind? What am I refusing to see?

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 8 | Produces reflection framework and questions |
| Invocable | 7 | "Check my intellectual honesty" "Am I being honest with myself?" |
| Scoped | 9 | Focused on self-examination |
| Reusable | 8 | Any belief or position one holds strongly |
| Valuable | 8 | Valuable but requires user commitment to use honestly |
| **TOTAL** | **40** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Am I being intellectually honest?"
- "Check my reasoning for bias"
- "What am I refusing to see?"
- "Apply Orwell's honesty test to my position"

### 6. Plain English Translator

**Description:** Takes complex, jargon-laden, or bureaucratic text and rewrites it in simple, direct language anyone can understand. Applies Orwell's preference for Anglo-Saxon words over Latinate, active voice over passive, and concrete over abstract.

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces rewritten plain English version |
| Invocable | 9 | "Put this in plain English" "Simplify this" |
| Scoped | 10 | Focused specifically on translation to plain language |
| Reusable | 10 | Legal, medical, technical, academic, bureaucratic contexts |
| Valuable | 8 | Useful but overlaps with Clarity Audit |
| **TOTAL** | **47** | **HIGH PRIORITY** |

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Put this in plain English"
- "Simplify this for a general audience"
- "Remove the jargon"
- "Make this accessible"
