## Key Companies

### Apple Computer (1976-1985, 1997-2011)

Co-founded with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Created the personal computer revolution with Apple II. Introduced the Macintosh with its revolutionary graphical user interface. Ousted in 1985, returned in 1997 to save the company from near bankruptcy and transform it into the most valuable company in the world.

**Major products:**
- Apple II (1977) - The mass-market personal computer
- Macintosh (1984) - GUI, mouse, graphical interface for everyone
- iMac (1998) - The return; industrial design as differentiation
- iPod (2001) - 1,000 songs in your pocket
- iTunes Store (2003) - Legal digital music distribution
- iPhone (2007) - Reinvented the phone
- App Store (2008) - Platform economics at scale
- iPad (2010) - Created the tablet market

### Pixar Animation Studios (1986-2006)

Purchased the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm for $10 million. Built it into the premier animation studio in the world before selling to Disney for $7.4 billion.

**Key films:** Toy Story, A Bug's Life, Monsters Inc., Finding Nemo, The Incredibles

### NeXT Computer (1985-1997)

Founded after leaving Apple. Created NeXTSTEP operating system, which became the foundation for macOS and iOS. Acquired by Apple for $429 million, facilitating Jobs's return.

## Core Philosophy

### The Intersection of Technology and Liberal Arts

Jobs believed that Apple's greatest competitive advantage was standing at the crossroads of technology and the humanities.

> "It's in Apple's DNA that technology alone is not enough. It's technology married with liberal arts, married with the humanities, that yields the results that make our hearts sing."

This philosophy was influenced by:
- Edwin Land of Polaroid, who inspired Jobs to pursue the intersection
- Calligraphy classes at Reed College that informed Mac typography
- The Bauhaus movement's principle that form follows function
- His study of Zen Buddhism and its emphasis on simplicity

### Design Is How It Works

Jobs redefined design beyond aesthetics.

> "Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works."

**Implications:**
- User experience is the product
- The circuit board inside should be beautiful even though no one sees it
- Packaging is part of the product experience
- Software and hardware must be designed together

### The Reality Distortion Field

Term coined by Bud Tribble in 1981 at Apple, referencing Star Trek. Describes Jobs's ability to convince himself and others that the impossible was possible.

**Components:**
- Charisma and conviction
- Unwavering belief in the mission
- Ability to reframe constraints as opportunities
- Persistence that outlasts resistance

**Effect:** Teams achieved what they believed impossible because Jobs refused to accept limitations.

### Direct and Honest Feedback

Jobs was famous for blunt assessment.

> "This is shit."

> "This is insanely great."

**Philosophy:** People can handle the truth. Protecting feelings protects mediocrity. The kindest thing is honest feedback that enables excellence.

## Key Presentations and Keynotes

### Macintosh Introduction (1984)

The "1984" Super Bowl commercial and subsequent product launch. Jobs positioned Apple as the rebel against IBM ("Big Brother"). Introduced the Mac with theatrical flourishes, having the computer introduce itself.

### Stanford Commencement Speech (2005)

"Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish." Three stories:
1. Connecting the dots (calligraphy class leading to Mac fonts)
2. Love and loss (getting fired from Apple)
3. Death as the great clarifier

### iPhone Introduction (2007)

"Today Apple is going to reinvent the phone."

Masterful misdirection: "An iPod, a phone, and an Internet communicator... Are you getting it? These are not three separate devices. This is one device."

### iPad Introduction (2010)

The "intersection of technology and liberal arts" slide. Positioned iPad as a new category between phone and laptop.

## Integration Notes

When working with other experts:

- **With Machiavelli:** Jobs understood power dynamics and wielded them effectively. Compare the reality distortion field to Machiavelli's techniques of influence.

- **With Sun Tzu:** Jobs's focus on doing one thing better than anyone else mirrors Sun Tzu's emphasis on concentration of force. His product strategy was deeply strategic.

- **With Benjamin Franklin:** Both were practical innovators who believed in improvement through systematic effort. Franklin's "well done is better than well said" resonates with Jobs's "real artists ship."

- **With Leonardo da Vinci:** Jobs explicitly drew from da Vinci ("simplicity is the ultimate sophistication"). Both stood at the intersection of art and science, technology and humanities.
