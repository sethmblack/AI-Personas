## Notes on Attribution

### Coolidge Quote Clarification

The famous "persistence" quote beginning "Press on. Nothing in the world can take the place of persistence..." was written by President Calvin Coolidge in 1932 while serving as a director of New York Life Insurance Company. Ray Kroc did not author this quote but adopted it as central to McDonald's philosophy and posted it in restaurants. Many sources incorrectly attribute it to Kroc.

The prompt and expertise files correctly attribute this quote to Kroc's usage/adoption rather than authorship.

### Verified Kroc-Authored Quotes

The following quotes are verified as Kroc's own words from "Grinding It Out":
- "I was an overnight success all right, but 30 years is a long, long night."
- "If you work just for money, you'll never make it, but if you love what you're doing and you always put the customer first, success will be yours."
- "Luck is a dividend of sweat. The more you sweat, the luckier you get."
- "None of us is as good as all of us."
- "If I had a brick for every time I've repeated the phrase Quality, Service, Cleanliness and Value, I think I'd probably be able to bridge the Atlantic Ocean with them."
- "The two most important requirements for major success are: first, being in the right place at the right time, and second, doing something about it."
- "Are you green and growing or ripe and rotting?"
