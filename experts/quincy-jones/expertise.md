## Career Overview

Quincy Jones's career spanned seven decades and virtually every form of popular music. He was one of the few producers to have #1 records in three consecutive decades (1960s, 1970s, 1980s). His work touched jazz, pop, R&B, hip-hop, film scoring, and television.

### Career Phases

**1950s: Jazz Foundation**
- Studied at Berklee College of Music (then Schillinger House)
- Played trumpet and arranged for Lionel Hampton's band
- Met and befriended Ray Charles as teenagers
- Toured as musical director for Dizzy Gillespie's State Department tour
- Freelance arranger for Count Basie, Sarah Vaughan, Duke Ellington, Dinah Washington

**1960s: Pop Production & Film Scoring**
- First #1 hit producing Lesley Gore's "It's My Party" (1963)
- First film score: *The Pawnbroker* (1964)
- Major film scores: *In the Heat of the Night*, *In Cold Blood*, *The Italian Job*
- Arranged for Frank Sinatra and Count Basie (*It Might as Well Be Swing*, *Sinatra at the Sands*)
- First Black composer embraced by Hollywood establishment

**1970s: Television & Expansion**
- TV themes: "Sanford and Son," "Ironside" (first synthesizer-based TV theme)
- Emmy Award for *Roots* (1977)
- Produced *The Wiz* (1978), where he met Michael Jackson

**1980s: Pop Dominance**
- Produced Michael Jackson's *Off the Wall* (1979), *Thriller* (1982), *Bad* (1987)
- *Thriller* became best-selling album of all time (65+ million copies)
- Organized and produced "We Are the World" (1985)
- Co-produced *The Color Purple* (1985)

**1990s-2020s: Legacy & Mentorship**
- Produced *The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air* (1990)
- Founded Quincy Jones Entertainment
- Mentored emerging artists including Jacob Collier
- Continued cross-genre collaborations (*Back on the Block*, 1989)
- Published *12 Notes: On Life and Creativity* (2022)

## Key Collaborators

### Frank Sinatra
- First worked together in 1958 for Monaco charity concert
- Arranged *It Might as Well Be Swing* (1964) with Count Basie Orchestra
- Conducted *Sinatra at the Sands* (1966)
- Produced Sinatra's final studio album *L.A. Is My Lady* (1984)
- "Fly Me to the Moon" arrangement played on Apollo 11 moon landing
- Remained friends until Sinatra's death in 1998; Sinatra left Jones his ring

### Michael Jackson
- Met during production of *The Wiz* (1978)
- Jackson asked Jones to recommend producers; Jones offered himself
- Produced three consecutive albums that redefined pop music
- Worked through 30 songs to select 9 for *Thriller*
- Used 62 musicians and 22 singers on *Thriller*
- Jones recognized Jackson's "burning desire for perfection and unlimited ambition"

### Count Basie
- "Practically adopted" Jones at age 13
- Lifelong mentor, manager, father figure, friend
- Jones eventually conducted for Basie
- Won first Grammy for Basie arrangement of "I Can't Stop Loving You" (1963)

### Ray Charles
- Met as teenagers in Seattle when Charles was unknown
- Became lifelong friends
- Early collaborators before either achieved fame

### Key Production Team
- **Bruce Swedien** - Engineer on Michael Jackson albums
- **Rod Temperton** - Songwriter ("Rock With You," "Thriller," "Off the Wall")
- **Nadia Boulanger** - Composition teacher in Paris

## Core Production Philosophy

### The Producer's Total Responsibility

"The producer has to be able to take charge of virtually every phase of the creative process. He or she must be able to find and recognize a good song, get the right instrumentalists and background singers, and find the right engineer and studio. You have to be the conductor of everything from the bottom to the top of the project."

### Genre-Fluid Approach

Jones defined himself by refusing genre boundaries. His work seamlessly blended jazz, funk, R&B, pop, and hip-hop. *Back on the Block* (1989) brought together jazz icons and young hip-hop artists, demonstrating that music could transcend cultural divides.

"I like all music. The only music I don't like is bad music."

### Science and Soul

"Creativity is made up of two parts: science and soul. The scientific side is that which needs to be learned and practiced. But the soulful side is composed of emotions - something that can't be taught. It's simply the essence of who you are as a human being."

### The 12 Notes Principle

From his teacher Nadia Boulanger: "Quincy, there are only twelve notes. Until God gives us thirteen, I want you to know what everybody did with those twelve." Bach, Beethoven, Bo Diddley - everyone works with the same constraints. Excellence comes from what you do within them.

## The 12 Notes Framework (from his book)

Jones structured his 2022 book *12 Notes: On Life and Creativity* around 12 principles, each linked to a musical note:

1. **Recycle your pain** - Transform suffering into creative fuel
2. **If you can see it, you can be it** - Visualization precedes achievement
3. **Go to know** - Seek knowledge actively
4. **Establish your guideposts** - Set meaningful goals
5. **Always be prepared for a great opportunity** - Preparation meets luck
6. **Sharpen your left brain** - Master the craft, the science
7. **Avoid paralysis from analysis** - Don't overthink into inaction
8. **Understand the power of being underestimated** - Use low expectations as advantage
9. **Do what's never been done before** - Innovation requires courage
10. **Value relationships** - People over transactions
11. **Trust your gut** - Intuition from experience
12. **Recognize the beauty and inherent value of life** - Gratitude and presence
