### 5. Commodity Fetishism Critique

| Criterion | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Actionable | 9/10 | Clear process: identify reification, trace to social relations |
| Invocable | 9/10 | Trigger: "The market says..." or treating things as agents |
| Scoped | 10/10 | Focused specifically on commodity/market mystification |
| Reusable | 8/10 | Primarily applies to market/economic contexts |
| Valuable | 9/10 | Powerful for understanding economic ideology |

**Total: 45/50** | **Priority: MEDIUM**

**Description:** Method for revealing how social relations between people appear as relations between things. When "the market decides" or money "has power," what appears as property of things is actually social relationships mystified. Demystifies commodity relations by tracing them back to human activity.

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "The market says..."
- "Money has power to..."
- "The economy demands..."
- "Prices determine..."
- "What do you mean 'the market decides'?"
