## Key Frameworks to Apply

### The Market Creation Framework

Morita's approach to product development:

1. **Observe Behavior, Not Preferences** - Watch what people do, not what they say they want
2. **Imagine Delight** - What would make users love this, not merely accept it?
3. **Trust Intuition** - When research conflicts with observation, trust observation
4. **Commit Fully** - Bold bets require bold commitment
5. **Learn from Failure** - Wrong intuitions teach what surveys cannot

**Technical Application:**
- Developer experience design based on observed workflows
- Feature development beyond explicit requests
- Platform decisions trusting engineering intuition
- User research through shadowing, not surveys

### The Global-First Design Principle

Design for worldwide appeal from the beginning:

1. **Universal Naming** - Names pronounceable and meaningful across languages
2. **Cultural Neutrality** - Avoid elements that require localization
3. **Modular Localization** - Core unchanged, surfaces adaptable
4. **Market Presence** - Understand markets from within, not from headquarters
5. **Consistent Quality** - Same standard everywhere, no regional compromise

**Technical Application:**
- Internationalization (i18n) built in from start
- API design for global developer consumption
- Documentation in international English
- Time zone and locale handling as first-class concerns

### The Brand Prominence Strategy

Protect and display the company name:

1. **Name on Everything** - Every product carries the brand visibly
2. **No Anonymous Manufacturing** - Never build for others to rebrand
3. **Quality Association** - Each product reinforces brand meaning
4. **Reputation Building** - Consistent excellence over time
5. **Perception Patience** - Allow reputation to catch up with reality

**Technical Application:**
- Open source projects maintaining company identity
- APIs and SDKs carrying organizational branding
- Documentation and tooling as brand touchpoints
- Long-term thinking about developer community perception

## Morita on Innovation

### On Market Research

"We don't believe in market research for a new product unknown to the public. So we never do any. We are the experts."

**Application:** User research shows what exists; innovation creates what doesn't. Both have value, but they answer different questions.

### On Risk-Taking

"When I asked the engineering team to create the Walkman, they said it wouldn't sell. I said if we couldn't sell 30,000 units, I would resign as president."

**Application:** Conviction requires commitment. Half-hearted innovation produces half-hearted results.

### On Understanding Markets

"To understand the American market, I had to live in America. I moved my family to New York."

**Application:** Remote analysis produces remote understanding. Immersion produces insight.

### On Quality

"Our products must be the best. If we cannot make something the best, we should not make it at all."

**Application:** Mediocre products damage reputation more than missing products.

## Morita's Method for Product Development

### Phase 1: Observation
- Watch how people actually behave
- Notice frustrations and workarounds
- Identify unmet needs people can't articulate

### Phase 2: Imagination
- Envision what would delight users
- Think beyond current limitations
- Design for joy, not just function

### Phase 3: Commitment
- Bet on the vision
- Override cautious engineering
- Accept personal accountability

### Phase 4: Execution
- Build to the highest quality
- Ensure global readiness
- Protect the brand in every detail

### Phase 5: Learning
- If successful, understand why
- If failed, understand why
- Apply learning to next product
