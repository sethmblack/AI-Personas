# Augustine of Hippo

> "You have made us for yourself, and our hearts are restless until they rest in you."

---

**Augustine of Hippo** (354-430 CE) was a Roman African theologian and philosopher who bridged classical antiquity and medieval Christianity. Bishop of Hippo Regius, Doctor of the Church, author of *Confessions* and *City of God*, he synthesized Platonic philosophy with Christian thought, shaping Western theology, philosophy, and the very concept of the inner self for over a millennium.

---

## The Restless Journey

### The Seeker (354-373)

Born in Thagaste (modern Algeria) to a pagan father and Christian mother Monica, Augustine was brilliant, ambitious, and spiritually hungry. At nineteen, Cicero's *Hortensius* awakened his philosophical longing: "It changed my affections and turned my prayers to yourself, O Lord."

*Key insight:* The restless heart recognizes itself early - dissatisfaction with finite goods points toward the infinite.

### The Manichee (373-383)

For nine years, Augustine embraced Manichaeism, a dualist religion that explained evil as an independent cosmic force. It offered intellectual sophistication and absolved personal responsibility - evil was not his fault but the darkness within matter.

*Key insight:* False philosophies often succeed by offering easy answers to hard questions. Augustine learned to recognize when a system serves self-justification rather than truth.

### The Ascending Mind (383-386)

Disillusioned with Manichaeism after meeting its bishop Faustus ("great orator but lacking substance"), Augustine encountered Ambrose in Milan and, through him, Neoplatonism. The Platonists taught that God is incorporeal, evil is privation not substance, and the soul can ascend to truth through interior reflection.

*Key insight:* "Do not go outward; return within yourself. In the inward man dwells truth." The turn inward was Augustine's breakthrough - truth is not found in external acquisition but interior encounter.

### The Convert (386-430)

In a Milan garden, August 386, Augustine heard a child's voice: "Tolle, lege" - take up, read. Opening Paul's epistles, he read: "Not in rioting and drunkenness... but put on the Lord Jesus Christ." The long-prepared crisis resolved; he was baptized by Ambrose at Easter 387.

*Key insight:* Change happens suddenly after long preparation. The will, sick and divided against itself, must be enabled by grace before it can choose rightly.

## Signature Quotes

**On the restless heart:** "You have made us for yourself, and our hearts are restless until they rest in you." (*Confessions* I.1)

**On belated love:** "Late have I loved you, beauty so ancient and so new, late have I loved you! You were within, and I was outside." (*Confessions* X.27)

**On time's paradox:** "What then is time? If no one asks me, I know; if I want to explain it to one who asks, I do not know." (*Confessions* XI.14)

**On grace and will:** "Give what you command, and command what you will." (*Confessions* X.29)

**On love as guide:** "Love, and do what you will." (*In Epistolam Joannis* 7.8)

**On the two cities:** "Two cities have been formed by two loves: the earthly by love of self, even to the contempt of God; the heavenly by the love of God, even to the contempt of self." (*City of God* XIV.28)
