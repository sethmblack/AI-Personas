## HIGH Priority

### 1. authentic-storytelling

**Source Pattern:** The Bourdain Methodology for Telling Stories
**Purpose:** Transform any subject into a compelling human story by finding the real people and their true experiences behind abstractions.
**Trigger:** "Tell this story authentically" or "Find the human angle" or "How do I make this compelling?"
**Inputs:** Subject matter, context, available access to people/places
**Outputs:** Narrative approach with specific human subjects, sensory details, honest framing, and emotional resonance
**Reasoning:** This is Bourdain's core contribution to documentary and writing. His five-step methodology (lead with the human, show don't explain, build trust, allow silence, honor the unseen) is concrete and reusable. Transforms abstract topics into visceral stories.

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: YES - 5 explicit steps with clear guidance
- Invocable: YES - natural trigger phrases exist
- Scoped: YES - one responsibility (finding and framing authentic narratives)
- Reusable: YES - applies to journalism, content creation, marketing, personal writing
- Valuable: YES - prevents generic, soulless content

**Score: 48/50**

### 2. cultural-immersion-method

**Source Pattern:** Humble Ignorance + Going-Native Approach + The Shared Table
**Purpose:** Learn about an unfamiliar culture, place, or community with genuine respect and depth, avoiding tourist-trap superficiality.
**Trigger:** "How do I really understand this place/culture?" or "I'm visiting [place] and want authentic experience" or "How do I connect across cultural differences?"
**Inputs:** Destination/community to understand, constraints (time, access), goals
**Outputs:** Practical approach for immersion, specific behaviors to adopt, mindset shifts required, warning signs of superficiality
**Reasoning:** Bourdain's methodology for approaching unfamiliar cultures is systematic and replicable. The humble ignorance starting position + practical guidance (follow locals, eat breakfast, sit at the counter) creates a complete framework for cross-cultural engagement.

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: YES - specific behaviors and mindsets prescribed
- Invocable: YES - travel and cultural questions trigger it
- Scoped: YES - cultural learning and connection
- Reusable: YES - any travel, cross-cultural work, or community engagement
- Valuable: YES - prevents shallow tourism and cultural missteps

**Score: 46/50**

### 3. bullshit-detection

**Source Pattern:** Anti-Pretension Philosophy + Honest Self-Reflection
**Purpose:** Identify and call out pretension, inauthenticity, and empty sophistication in any domain.
**Trigger:** "Is this authentic or pretentious?" or "Cut through the bullshit" or "What's really going on here?"
**Inputs:** Situation, product, experience, or claim to evaluate
**Outputs:** Honest assessment distinguishing genuine quality from performance, specific indicators of authenticity vs. pretension
**Reasoning:** Bourdain's most distinctive quality was his ability to identify what was real versus what was posturing. His framework (craft over credentials, character over technique, passion over polish) provides clear evaluation criteria applicable far beyond food.

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: YES - clear criteria for evaluation
- Invocable: YES - strong trigger phrases
- Scoped: YES - authenticity assessment
- Reusable: YES - restaurants, products, people, experiences, organizations
- Valuable: YES - saves time/money, prevents bad decisions

**Score: 44/50**

### 4. honest-feedback-framework

**Source Pattern:** Kitchen Truth + "Tell them what they need to hear"
**Purpose:** Deliver difficult feedback with honesty and respect, prioritizing the recipient's growth over their comfort.
**Trigger:** "How do I give honest feedback?" or "I need to tell someone something they won't want to hear" or "How do I critique without being cruel?"
**Inputs:** Feedback to deliver, relationship context, stakes involved
**Outputs:** Structured feedback approach balancing directness with humanity, specific language choices, follow-up actions
**Reasoning:** Bourdain's kitchen background created a distinctive approach to feedback—direct, immediate, focused on improvement, but rooted in respect for the craft and the person. His "never accept anything mediocre" philosophy combined with genuine care for his team offers a model.

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: YES - specific principles and approach
- Invocable: YES - feedback situations trigger it
- Scoped: YES - giving difficult feedback
- Reusable: YES - professional, creative, personal contexts
- Valuable: YES - prevents feedback avoidance and relationship damage

**Score: 42/50**
