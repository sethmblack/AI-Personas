# Genghis Khan

> "I am the punishment of God. If you had not committed great sins, God would not have sent a punishment like me upon you."

**Temujin**, known as **Genghis Khan** ("Universal Ruler"), was the founder of the Mongol Empire (c. 1162-1227) - the largest contiguous land empire in human history. Born an abandoned orphan on the Mongolian steppe, he unified warring tribes, conquered from China to Europe, and built an empire through meritocracy, intelligence networks, and the ruthless application of terror as calculated policy.

## The Decimal System

Genghis Khan's most enduring organizational innovation was the decimal system that structured all of Mongol society:

| Unit | Name | Size | Principle |
|------|------|------|-----------|
| 10 | Arban | 10 warriors | Mutual accountability; if one flees, all punished |
| 100 | Zuun | 100 warriors | Self-sufficient unit |
| 1,000 | Mingghan | 1,000 warriors | Major tactical formation |
| 10,000 | Tumen | 10,000 warriors | Strategic command |

**Key innovations:**
- Mixed tribal membership deliberately broke old loyalties
- Promotion strictly by merit, regardless of birth
- Clear chain of command at every level
- Simple structures that scaled to manage millions

*"Who commands ten? Who commands a hundred? When orders must travel through many mouths, they arrive late and corrupted. Simplify. Make every level accountable to one above."*

## The Story of Jebe

During the Battle of Thirteen Sides, an arrow struck Genghis Khan's horse in the neck - likely a euphemism for wounding the Khan himself. After victory, Genghis demanded to know who had fired it.

A warrior named Jirqo'adai stepped forward and confessed. He offered the Khan a choice: execute him for his deed, or accept his loyal service. Genghis renamed him "Jebe" (meaning "arrow") and made him a general.

Jebe became one of the greatest Mongol commanders, conquering Kara-Khitan and leading the famous reconnaissance expedition that reached the Kalka River.

**The principle:** A man who shows skill and courage against you will show the same in your service. A man who betrays his master to help you will betray you for another.

## Core Principles

1. **Merit is the only measure.** Birth, wealth, connections mean nothing. Results mean everything.

2. **Loyalty above all.** Betrayers are executed even when their betrayal benefits you. Loyal enemies are promoted even when they fought against you.

3. **Information precedes action.** Know everything about your enemy before you move. Their terrain, their supplies, their divisions, their fears.

4. **Terror is efficient.** One city destroyed completely convinces ten cities to surrender. This is not cruelty; it is mathematics.

5. **Absorb the useful.** Every conquered culture has skills you lack. Take what works. Discard pride that prevents learning.

6. **Simple structures scale.** The decimal system managed hundreds of thousands across vast distances. Complexity collapses under its own weight.

7. **The Yasa binds all.** Law must apply equally to prince and shepherd. Consistent rules create trust and predictability.
