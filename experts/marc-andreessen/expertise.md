## Background

**Full Name:** Marc Lowell Andreessen
**Born:** July 9, 1971, Cedar Falls, Iowa
**Education:** BS in Computer Science, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (1993)
**Nationality:** American
**Profession:** Software engineer, entrepreneur, investor

### Career Timeline

**1992-1993: NCSA Mosaic**
- While at University of Illinois, co-authored Mosaic with Eric Bina at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA)
- Mosaic was the first widely-used web browser with inline images
- Released in 1993, it brought the World Wide Web to mainstream users
- Graduated from UIUC in 1993

**1994: The Netscape Founding**
- Received email from Jim Clark, founder of Silicon Graphics Inc.
- Clark believed the Mosaic browser had great commercial possibilities
- In April 1994, Andreessen and Clark flew to Illinois and recruited the original NCSA Mosaic team
- Clark typed out six offer letters in his hotel room; all six engineers accepted on the spot
- Founded Mosaic Communications Corporation with $4 million of Clark's money

**1994-1999: Netscape**
- Company renamed to Netscape Communications due to University of Illinois trademark concerns
- Netscape Navigator 0.9 released October 13, 1994
- Within four months, Navigator captured three-quarters of the browser market
- IPO on August 9, 1995 - stock opened at $28, closed at $58.25
- Andreessen appeared on cover of Time magazine
- Netscape launched the dot-com era and the "browser wars" with Microsoft
- AOL acquired Netscape for $4.2 billion in 1999

**1999-2007: Post-Netscape Ventures**
- Co-founded Loudcloud (1999), an early cloud computing company
- Loudcloud pivoted to enterprise software as Opsware
- HP acquired Opsware for $1.6 billion in 2007
- Co-founded Ning (2004), a social networking platform

**2009-Present: Andreessen Horowitz (a16z)**
- Co-founded with Ben Horowitz in July 2009
- Started with $300 million fund
- Pioneered the "founder-friendly" VC model
- Notable investments: Facebook, Twitter, Airbnb, GitHub, Slack, Lyft, Pinterest, Stripe, Coinbase, Clubhouse, Roblox
- AUM grew to over $35 billion
- Expanded into crypto (2021), bio (2022), and games (2022) dedicated funds

### Board Positions
- Meta/Facebook (2008-present)
- HP Enterprise (2014-2018)
- eBay (2008-2014)

## Major Essays and Key Ideas

### "Software Is Eating the World" (Wall Street Journal, August 20, 2011)

**Core thesis:** Software companies are poised to take over large swaths of the economy.

**Key passages:**
- "Six decades into the computer revolution, four decades since the invention of the microprocessor, and two decades into the rise of the modern Internet, all of the technology required to transform industries through software finally works and can be widely delivered at global scale."
- "More and more major businesses and industries are being run on software and delivered as online services - from movies to agriculture to national defense."
- "Companies in every industry need to assume that a software revolution is coming. This includes even industries that are software-based today."

**Examples cited:**
- Amazon: "Perhaps the single most dramatic example of this phenomenon of software eating a traditional business is the suicide of Borders and corresponding rise of Amazon."
- Automotive: "In today's cars, software runs the engines, controls safety features, entertains passengers, guides drivers to destinations and connects each car to mobile, satellite and GPS networks."
- Netflix, Spotify, LinkedIn, Square

**On tech valuations:** "Today's stock market actually hates technology, as shown by all-time low price/earnings ratios for major public technology companies."

**Warning:** "No one should expect building a new high-growth, software-powered company in an established industry to be easy. It's brutally difficult."

### "It's Time to Build" (a16z, April 18, 2020)

**Context:** Published during COVID-19 pandemic

**Core thesis:** America and the Western world have lost the ability to build things.

**Key passages:**
- "Every Western institution was unprepared for the coronavirus pandemic, despite many prior warnings. This monumental failure of institutional effectiveness will reverberate for the rest of the decade."
- "The problem is what we didn't do in advance, and what we're failing to do now. And that is a failure of action, and specifically our widespread inability to build."
- "We don't have enough coronavirus tests, or test materials - including, amazingly, cotton swabs and common reagents. We don't have enough ventilators, negative pressure rooms, and ICU beds. And we don't have enough surgical masks, eye shields, and medical gowns - as I write this, New York City has put out a desperate call for rain ponchos to be used as medical gowns. Rain ponchos! In 2020!"

**On housing:** "We can't build nearly enough housing in our cities with surging economic potential - which results in crazily skyrocketing housing prices in places like San Francisco, making it nearly impossible for regular people to move in and take the jobs of the future."

**On politics:**
- "The right is generally pro production, but is too often corrupted by forces that hold back market-based competition and the building of things. The right must fight hard against crony capitalism, regulatory capture, ossified oligopolies."
- "The left starts out with a stronger bias toward the public sector in many of these areas. To which I say, prove the superior model!"

**The problem:** "The problem is desire. We need to want these things. The problem is inertia. We need to want these things more than we want to prevent these things."

**Closing:** "Our nation and our civilization were built on production, on building. There is only one way to honor their legacy and to create the future we want for our own children and grandchildren, and that's to build."

### "The Techno-Optimist Manifesto" (a16z, October 16, 2023)

**Format:** 5,200-word essay with 108 statements, repeating "We believe" 113 times, divided into 15 sections

**Core thesis:** Technology is the primary driver of human progress; pessimism about technology is wrong and counterproductive.

**Key beliefs:**
- "Techno-Optimists believe that societies, like sharks, grow or die. We believe growth is progress - leading to vitality, expansion of life, increasing knowledge, higher well being."
- "There are only three sources of growth: population growth, natural resource utilization, and technology. Developed societies are depopulating all over the world, natural resource utilization has sharp limits, and so the only perpetual source of growth is technology."

**On AI:** "Artificial Intelligence is our alchemy, our Philosopher's Stone - we are literally making sand think. We are poised for an intelligence takeoff that will expand our capabilities to unimagined heights."

**On human agency:** "We believe that we are, have been, and will always be the masters of technology, not mastered by technology. Victim mentality is a curse in every domain of life, including in our relationship with technology."

**On population:** Claims the planet is "dramatically underpopulated" and believes global population can "quite easily expand to 50 billion people or more."

**"The Enemy":** Lists "stagnation, anti-merit, anti-ambition, anti-striving, anti-achievement, anti-greatness, statism, authoritarianism, collectivism, central planning, socialism" as enemies of Techno-Optimism.

**Patron Saints:** Lists 56 people including Julian Simon, Milton Friedman, Thomas Sowell, and others.

### The Pmarca Guide to Startups (2007)

Multi-part blog series that became foundational startup literature.

**Part 1: Why not to do a startup**
- Creating a startup is an emotional rollercoaster
- Nothing happens unless you make it happen
- You'll get told no - a lot
- Hiring is difficult
- Eventually you're going to have to hire executives

**Part 4: The only thing that matters (Product-Market Fit)**

**Definition:** "Being in a good market with a product that can satisfy that market."

**On its importance:** "The only thing that matters is getting to product/market fit."

**Signs you DON'T have it:**
- "The customers aren't quite getting much value out of the product/service"
- "Word of mouth isn't spreading"
- "Usage isn't growing that fast"
- "Press reviews are kind of 'blah'"
- "The sales cycle takes too long"
- "Most of the deals never close"

**Signs you DO have it:**
- "You can always feel when Product/Market Fit is happening"
- "You're hiring sales and customer support staff as fast as you can"
- "Reporters are calling because they've heard about your hot new thing"
- "The market is pulling product out of your hands"
- "If you have to ask whether you have it, you don't"

**What to do:** "Do whatever is required to get to product/market fit. Including changing out people, rewriting your product, moving into a different market, telling customers no when you don't want to, telling customers yes when you don't want to, raising that fourth round of highly dilutive venture capital - whatever is required."

**The Rachleff/Andreessen formulation:**
- "When a great team meets a lousy market, market wins"
- "When a lousy team meets a great market, market wins"
- "When a great team meets a great market, something special happens"

**Startup lifecycle:** "The life of any startup can be divided into two parts: before product/market fit (call this 'BPMF') and after product/market fit ('APMF')."

## Investment Philosophy

### The a16z Model

**Core insight:** "The great part of venture capitalism is that picking is the small part of the game. So much of what a venture capitalist firm is are its relationships with the world."

**On prepared mind:** "You want to have as much 'prepared mind' as you possibly can. Learn as much as you can about as many things, as much as you can. You want to enter as close as you can to a zen-like blank slate of perfect humility at the beginning of the meeting saying 'teach me.'"

**Power law returns:** "The key characteristic of venture capital is that returns are a power-law distribution. There are about 4,000 startups a year that are founded in the technology industry which would like to raise venture capital and we can invest in about 20."

**On bravery:** "Founders want to work with investors who are brave."

### The Founder-Friendly Model

a16z's biggest bet was on a sociocultural shift in founder-investor relationships:
- Insisted that founders, especially technical visionaries, should run their own companies
- In 2009, bucked the popular trend of replacing founders with professional managers
- General partners must have operating experience running a company
- "You get somebody on the board who has been through the war and really understands what things are like"

### Platform Model

Over 60 full-time professionals organized across 5 operating teams:
- Recruiting support
- Marketing/communications
- Sales expertise
- Go-to-market strategy
- Network connections

## Key Patterns in Thinking

### Technology Inevitability
Certain technologies are inevitable once the components exist. The question is timing and execution, not whether.

### Market Over Team
A great market can make an average team successful. A great team in a bad market will fail. Market is the most important variable.

### Building as Moral Imperative
The correct response to problems is building solutions. Criticism without building is morally inferior to imperfect building.

### Cyclical Pessimism
Every generation thinks technology has gone too far or will destroy jobs/society. They're almost always wrong.

### Platform Shifts
Major opportunities emerge at platform transitions: mainframe to PC, PC to internet, internet to mobile, mobile to crypto/AI.

### Features vs. Products
Many startups are building features of larger platforms, not standalone products. Features get absorbed; products create companies.

## Debate Positions

### On Technology Regulation
- Generally opposes heavy regulation of tech
- Believes regulation often protects incumbents and slows innovation
- Supports free speech maximalism
- Critical of antitrust actions against tech companies

### On Crypto/Web3
- Strong advocate for cryptocurrency and blockchain technology
- a16z launched dedicated crypto fund
- Views crypto as the next major platform shift
- "Crypto represents the architecture for a new internet"

### On AI
- Highly bullish on artificial intelligence
- "AI will save the world" (2023 essay)
- Dismisses AI doom scenarios as overblown
- Believes AI will create abundance and solve major problems

### On Media
- Critical of mainstream tech coverage
- Believes journalists often miss the positive impacts of technology
- Has had public conflicts with various journalists
