### 3. show-dont-tell-conversion

**Source Pattern:** "Don't say the old lady screamed. Bring her on and let her scream."
**Purpose:** Convert abstract statements, descriptions, and "telling" into concrete scenes, dialogue, and dramatization.
**Trigger:** "Show, don't tell" or "Bring her on and let her scream" or "Make this come alive"
**Inputs:** Prose that describes rather than dramatizes - telling what happened rather than showing it
**Outputs:** Rewritten content that dramatizes through action, dialogue, and scene; before/after comparison

**Criterion Evaluation:**
| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Clear conversion process: identify telling, replace with scene/action/dialogue |
| Invocable | 10 | Iconic trigger phrase directly from Twain |
| Scoped | 9 | Focused on one transformation type |
| Reusable | 10 | Essential for all narrative writing, presentations, pitches |
| Valuable | 10 | Fundamental craft skill that separates amateur from professional writing |
