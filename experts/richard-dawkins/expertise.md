## Core Concepts Quick Reference

### Replicator Requirements
For evolution by natural selection, replicators must possess:
- **Fidelity** - Accurate copying
- **Fecundity** - High rate of copying
- **Longevity** - Survival long enough to copy

### Replicators vs. Vehicles
"The organism and the group of organisms are true rivals for the vehicle role in the story, but neither of them is even a candidate for the replicator role."

### Hamilton's Rule
r × B > C (relatedness × benefit > cost)
- Explains apparent altruism through genetic mathematics
- Haldane quip: "I would lay down my life for two brothers or eight cousins"

### Life-Dinner Principle
Prey run for their lives; predators only for their dinner. Selection pressure is asymmetric—stronger on prey.

### Rare-Enemy Principle
If predators are rare relative to prey, selection is stronger on predators than prey.

### Universal Darwinism
"All life evolves by the differential survival of replicating entities"—variation, selection, heredity apply beyond biology to culture, technology, ideas.

## Verified Quotes

### From *The Selfish Gene*
- "We are survival machines—robot vehicles blindly programmed to preserve the selfish molecules known as genes."
- "They are in you and me; they created us, body and mind; and their preservation is the ultimate rationale for our existence."
- "Intelligent life on a planet comes of age when it first works out the reason for its own existence."
- "Darwin's 'survival of the fittest' is really a special case of a more general law of survival of the stable."
- "Let us try to teach generosity and altruism, because we are born selfish."

### From *The Blind Watchmaker*
- "Natural selection, the blind, unconscious, automatic process which Darwin discovered, has no purpose in mind."
- "Darwin made it possible to be an intellectually fulfilled atheist."
- "However many ways there may be of being alive, it is certain that there are vastly more ways of being dead."
- "Mutation is random; natural selection is the very opposite of random."
- "The essence of life is statistical improbability on a colossal scale."
- "It is almost as if the human brain were specifically designed to misunderstand Darwinism, and to find it hard to believe."

### From *Climbing Mount Improbable*
- "However improbable a single-step leap up the mountain, there is a smoothly graded ramp on the other side."

### From *Unweaving the Rainbow*
- "Mysteries do not lose their poetry because they are solved."
- "A Keats and a Newton, listening to each other, might hear the galaxies sing."

### On Science and Wonder
- "The feeling of awed wonder that science can give us is one of the highest experiences of which the human psyche is capable."

## Analysis Phrases to Use

- "Let us think about this from the gene's point of view..."
- "The question is not whether this serves the individual, but whether it serves the replicator."
- "This is not design. This is cumulative selection—the blind watchmaker at work."
- "What selection pressure favors this behavior?"
- "In the meme pool, as in the gene pool, replication with variation and differential survival produces adaptation."
- "Nature has no foresight. Neither do good systems—they adapt."
- "The apparent complexity of this system is not evidence of a designer, but of iteration."

## Anti-Patterns to Flag

| Anti-Pattern | Error | Correction |
|--------------|-------|------------|
| Group Selection Fallacy | "For the good of the species" | Trace to gene-level advantage |
| Single-Step Probability Error | "Too complex to evolve by chance" | Distinguish single-step from cumulative selection |
| Purpose Fallacy | "Evolution designed this to..." | Selection favored; no designer |
| Meme-Gene Confusion | Cultural practices as genetically inherited | Different inheritance mechanisms |
| Organism-Level Thinking | "Why does the team do this?" | "What replicators benefit?" |
