## Key Works

### The Hero with a Thousand Faces - 1949
Campbell's magnum opus, introducing the concept of the "monomyth"â€”the universal pattern underlying hero myths across all cultures. Named by Time magazine as one of the 100 most influential books written in English since 1923.

**Core themes:**
- The monomyth (hero's journey)
- Departure, initiation, and return
- Universal archetypes across cultures
- Transformation through symbolic death and rebirth
- The boon and return to ordinary life

### The Masks of God (Four Volumes) - 1959-1968
Comprehensive study of world mythology across human history.
- *Primitive Mythology* (1959)
- *Oriental Mythology* (1962)
- *Occidental Mythology* (1964)
- *Creative Mythology* (1968)

### The Power of Myth - 1988 (posthumous)
Based on the famous PBS interview series with Bill Moyers filmed at George Lucas's Skywalker Ranch. Became one of the most popular TV series in public television history.

**Six episodes:**
1. The Hero's Adventure
2. The Message of the Myth
3. The First Storytellers
4. Sacrifice and Bliss
5. Love and the Goddess
6. Masks of Eternity

### Pathways to Bliss - 2004 (posthumous)
Collected lectures on mythology and personal transformation.

## Historical Context

### Campbell's Era
- Born into Irish Catholic family in New York
- Witnessed the Great Depression
- Taught through World War II and the Cold War era
- Influenced the counterculture movement of the 1960s-70s
- Died just before The Power of Myth aired, bringing his work to millions

### Key Influences on Campbell
- **Carl Jung** - Archetypes and the collective unconscious
- **Heinrich Zimmer** - Comparative approach to Eastern and Western myths
- **James Joyce** - Literary modernism, the term "monomyth" from Finnegans Wake
- **Sigmund Freud** - Psychological interpretation of symbols
- **Arnold Van Gennep** - Rites of passage (separation, initiation, return)
- **Native American traditions** - Early childhood fascination with indigenous myths

### Career at Sarah Lawrence College
- Professor of Literature from 1934-1972 (38 years)
- Developed his introductory mythology course that became The Hero with a Thousand Faces
- Known for passionate, engaging lectures

### Marriage to Jean Erdman
- Married dancer-choreographer Jean Erdman in 1938
- She was his former student at Sarah Lawrence
- 49 years of marriage until his death
- Together founded Theater of the Open Eye (1972)
- Jean lived until 2020 and founded the Joseph Campbell Foundation

- "Myths are clues to the spiritual potentialities of the human life."
- "Every religion is true one way or another. It is true when understood metaphorically."

## Cultural Impact

### George Lucas and Star Wars
George Lucas credited Campbell's work, particularly *The Hero with a Thousand Faces*, as a major influence on the Star Wars saga. Lucas became a friend and hosted the filming of *The Power of Myth* at Skywalker Ranch.

### Hollywood Screenwriting
Christopher Vogler's *The Writer's Journey: Mythic Structure for Writers* (1992) translated Campbell's hero's journey into a practical screenwriting guide, influencing countless films.

### Self-Help and Personal Development
"Follow your bliss" became a cultural touchstone, though often misinterpreted. Campbell influenced the positive psychology movement, though he would have rejected shallow interpretations.
