# Paul Graham - Expertise Research

## Background

**Full Name:** Paul Graham
**Born:** November 13, 1964
**Nationality:** English-American
**Profession:** Computer scientist, writer, essayist, entrepreneur, investor

### Career Highlights
- Created the programming language Arc
- Founded Viaweb (1995), later acquired by Yahoo and renamed Yahoo! Store
- Co-founded Y Combinator (2005) with Trevor Blackwell, Jessica Livingston, and Robert Morris
- Written 200+ essays on startups, programming, and thinking
- Y Combinator has funded 1,300+ startups including Reddit, Dropbox, Airbnb, Stripe, Twitch

### Recognition
- Technology journalist Steven Levy described Graham as a "hacker philosopher"
- One of the most influential voices in startup culture
- His essays have become required reading for founders

## Major Essays and Key Insights

### On Startups

**"How to Start a Startup" (2005)**
- The talk at Harvard Computer Society that launched Y Combinator
- Core message: Start with good people, make something customers want, spend as little money as possible

**"Startup = Growth"**
- A startup is a company designed to grow fast
- Growth rate is the defining characteristic, not the product or funding

**"Do Things That Don't Scale" (2013)**
- Founders must manually recruit first users
- Provide exceptional service to understand what users need
- "Recruit users individually rather than en masse"

**"How to Get Startup Ideas" (2012)**
- Look for problems, preferably your own
- Best ideas have three qualities: something founders want, can build themselves, few others realize are worth doing
- Turn off the "schlep filter" - don't avoid messy ideas

**"Founder Mode" (2024)**
- Challenges conventional wisdom about "hiring good people and giving them autonomy"
- Successful founders maintain deep involvement; "manager mode" often fails
- Brian Chesky's Airbnb turnaround as case study

### On Thinking and Ideas

**"How to Think for Yourself"**
- Conventional-minded vs. independent-minded people
- Independent thinkers are essential for startups
- Being contrarian isn't enough; you must be contrarian AND right

**"The Bus Ticket Theory of Genius" (2019)**
- Genius = ability + determination + obsessive interest
- The "bus ticket" quality: caring deeply about something for its own sake, not ulterior motives
- Larry Page became Larry Page because he was genuinely obsessed with search

**"Good and Bad Procrastination" (2005)**
- The best people are "terrible" procrastinators
- They avoid small tasks to focus on big important ones
- Key: what you're procrastinating ON matters more than procrastinating itself

### On Writing

**"Write Simply" (2021)**
- "Fancy writing doesn't just conceal ideas. It can also conceal the lack of them."
- Simple writing keeps you honest
- Write in spoken language

**"Write Like You Talk" (2015)**
- "Here's a simple trick for getting more people to read what you write: write in spoken language."
- Read drafts aloud; if it sounds weird, fix it

**"Writing, Briefly"**
- Write a bad version 1, then rewrite
- Cut anything unnecessary
- "Expect 80% of the ideas in an essay to happen after you start writing"

**"Putting Ideas into Words"**
- Writing is thinking
- "A good writer doesn't just think, and then write down what he thought. A good writer will always discover new things in the process of writing."

**"How to Write Usefully"**
- Useful writing = importance + novelty + correctness + strength
- Surprising the reader is the goal
- "Am I surprising myself?" is the test

### On Work and Career

**"Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule" (2009)**
- Makers need long, uninterrupted blocks of time
- Managers live by the hour
- A single meeting can destroy a maker's entire day
- Meetings cost makers more than managers realize

**"How to Do What You Love"**
- Prestige is like a "marker flag" for ambitious people
- "Don't be fooled by prestige. It's just the opinion of the average person."
- Do what you'd do even if you weren't paid

## Writing Style Analysis

### Characteristics

**Clarity Above All**
- Short sentences, simple words
- Eliminates jargon and unnecessary complexity
- "If something is hard to read, it's usually the writer's fault"

**Contrarian Insights**
- Takes conventional wisdom and inverts it
- "Startups are so weird that if you follow your instincts they will lead you astray"
- Opens essays with counterintuitive claims

**Concrete Examples**
- Illustrates every principle with specific examples
- Often draws from Y Combinator experience
- Uses historical and contemporary tech examples

**Thinking Out Loud**
- Essays feel like watching someone figure things out
- Admits uncertainty where appropriate
- "I think," "probably," "my guess is"

**Rewriting and Cutting**
- Multiple drafts, aggressive editing
- "Be quick to cut"
- "Half of essay writing is rereading"

### Voice Markers
- Direct address to reader
- Conversational but precise
- Short paragraphs (often single sentences)
- Uses footnotes for tangential points
- Delivers maxims naturally embedded in prose

## Debate Positions

### On Education
- Traditional education often kills curiosity
- Best learning happens when driven by genuine interest
- Universities are increasingly about credentialing, not learning

### On Big Companies
- Big companies optimize to avoid disasters, not to create breakthroughs
- Corporate life dampens both highs and lows
- Great people are often miserable in large organizations

### On VC/Funding
- Take as little as you can
- Maintain control as long as possible
- Most startups that fail have the failure mode of running out of money while searching for product-market fit

### On AI (Recent)
- AI represents a massive shift in how work gets done
- Founders should embrace AI tools early
- The best opportunities are in applying AI to specific domains
