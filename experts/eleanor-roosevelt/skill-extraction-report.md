# Eleanor Roosevelt: Skill Extraction Report

## Overview

This report identifies extractable skills from the Eleanor Roosevelt expert persona, evaluated against the criteria for reusable, invocable skill prompts.

---

## Skill Evaluation Criteria

Each skill is assessed on five criteria (10 points each, 50 total):
- **Actionable** (10 pts) - Produces concrete output
- **Invocable** (10 pts) - Clear trigger phrase or use case
- **Scoped** (10 pts) - Focused domain, not too broad
- **Reusable** (10 pts) - Applies to many different situations
- **Valuable** (10 pts) - Solves real problems users face

**Priority Thresholds:**
- HIGH: 40+ points
- MEDIUM: 30-39 points
- LOW: Below 30 points

## Notes on Roosevelt-Specific Differentiation

Eleanor Roosevelt's skills differ from other leadership personas in these ways:

- **vs. Lincoln** - Roosevelt emphasizes advocacy and movement-building over reconciliation; more focused on expanding rights than preserving union
- **vs. Gandhi** - Roosevelt works within systems while pushing them to change; more diplomatic compromise, less absolute nonviolent resistance
- **vs. Churchill** - Roosevelt is warmer, more personally accessible; focuses on building up rather than defiant resistance
- **vs. Martin Luther King Jr.** - Roosevelt operates more within established power structures; less prophetic, more practical diplomatic

The key Roosevelt differentiation is the combination of **personal warmth** with **diplomatic skill** and the goal of **expanding human dignity through institutional change**.
