# Nelson Mandela: Skill Extraction Report

## Extracted Skills

Based on analysis of Mandela's distinctive approaches to reconciliation, leadership, negotiation, and resilience, the following skills have been identified and scored.

---

## Skill 1: Reconciliation Framework

**Description:** Transform adversarial relationships into partnerships through understanding, symbolic action, and principled flexibility.

**Core Application:** When facing conflict where ongoing relationship matters more than winning, this framework guides users to transform enemies into partners rather than defeat them.

**Scoring:**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Produces concrete reconciliation strategy with specific steps |
| Invocable | 9 | Clear triggers: "How do I reconcile with..." or "Transform this conflict" |
| Scoped | 8 | Focused on relationship transformation, though applicable to various contexts |
| Reusable | 10 | Applies to interpersonal, organizational, and political conflicts |
| Valuable | 10 | Unique approach that goes beyond typical conflict resolution |

**Total: 47/50 - HIGH PRIORITY**

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "How do I reconcile with [adversary]?"
- "Transform this relationship"
- "Turn an enemy into a partner"
- "Move beyond this conflict"

## Skill 2: Strategic Patience Assessment

**Description:** Assess whether a situation requires patience or action, and develop discipline for long-term strategic thinking when immediate results are not possible.

**Core Application:** When users face situations where they want quick results but circumstances require sustained effort, this skill helps them develop the discipline and strategic clarity to persist effectively.

**Scoring:**

| Criterion | Score | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 9 | Produces patience/action assessment and long-term strategy |
| Invocable | 8 | Triggers: "Should I wait or act?" or "I'm frustrated by slow progress" |
| Scoped | 8 | Focused on patience vs. action decisions |
| Reusable | 9 | Applies to careers, projects, relationships, social change |
| Valuable | 9 | Addresses common failure mode of impatience in long-term endeavors |

**Total: 43/50 - HIGH PRIORITY**

**Trigger Phrases:**
- "Should I wait or act now?"
- "I'm frustrated by how slow this is going"
- "How do I maintain momentum on a long journey?"
- "Patience vs. action assessment"

| Reusable | 9 | Applies to any negotiation context |
