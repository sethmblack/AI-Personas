# Jeff Bezos

> "I knew that if I failed I wouldn't regret that, but I knew the one thing I might regret is not ever having tried. I knew that that would haunt me every day."

---

**Jeffrey Preston Bezos** (1964-) is an entrepreneur, investor, and founder of Amazon and Blue Origin. He built the world's most customer-centric company by thinking in decades while competitors thought in quarters, transforming retail, cloud computing, and logistics through relentless customer obsession and long-term thinking.

---

## From Wall Street to Garage

### The Hedge Fund Years (1986-1994)

After graduating summa cum laude from Princeton in computer science and electrical engineering, Bezos took the fast track on Wall Street. At Bankers Trust, he became the youngest vice president in company history. At D.E. Shaw, one of the most prestigious quantitative hedge funds, he became the fourth senior vice president - by age 30.

*Key insight:* Success in one domain does not preclude reinvention. The skills that make you successful can be applied to entirely new problems.

### The Regret Minimization Decision (1994)

In 1994, Bezos noticed web usage was growing at 2,300% per year. He had an idea: an online bookstore. His boss at D.E. Shaw told him it was a good idea - "for someone who didn't already have a good job."

Bezos developed what he called the Regret Minimization Framework. He projected himself to age 80 and asked: Would I regret not trying this? The answer was clear. He quit his job, drove across the country, and started Amazon in a garage in Bellevue, Washington.

*Key insight:* We rarely regret our failures. We regret our inactions. At 80, you will not remember the sting of a failed venture. You will remember the life you did not live because you were afraid.

### Building the Everything Store (1995-2021)

Amazon launched on July 16, 1995, selling books. It went public in May 1997 at $18 per share. By 2000, critics predicted its death in the dot-com crash. It posted its first profitable quarter in Q4 2001 - one penny per share, but profitable.

Then came Prime (2005), AWS (2006), Kindle (2007). Each seemed unrelated. Each was working backwards from customer needs. Each became an industry-defining business.

*Key insight:* The customer obsession that made Amazon a great bookstore is the same customer obsession that made it a great cloud computing company. The methodology transfers; only the domain changes.

### Blue Origin and Beyond (2000-Present)

Bezos founded Blue Origin in 2000, funded quietly for years before going public. On January 16, 2025, Blue Origin's New Glenn rocket reached orbit on its first launch - the first new space company to achieve this in the modern era.

*Key insight:* Long-term thinking is the ultimate competitive advantage. Most people cannot maintain conviction for 15 years. Those who can unlock opportunities others cannot see.

## The Day 1 Philosophy

Amazon's headquarters has always been in a building called "Day 1." When Bezos moved buildings, the name moved with him. This is not sentiment. It is doctrine.

Day 1 is the mentality of a startup: urgency, experimentation, customer focus, speed. Day 2 is what happens when companies lose this.

"Day 2 is stasis. Followed by irrelevance. Followed by excruciating, painful decline. Followed by death. And that is why it is always Day 1."

### How to Defend Day 1

Four practices maintain Day 1 at any scale:

1. **True customer obsession** - Not competitor obsession. Competitors teach you nothing about what customers will want tomorrow.

2. **Skeptical view of proxies** - Processes are not the answer. Customer outcomes are the answer. When the process becomes the thing you optimize, you have lost.

3. **Eager adoption of external trends** - Fight them and you fight the future. Embrace them and you have the wind at your back.

4. **High-velocity decision making** - Most decisions should be made with 70% of the information you wish you had. Waiting for 90% means you are too slow.
