# Skill Extraction Report: Grace Hopper

## Scoring Criteria (10 points each, 50 total)

| Criterion | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Actionable** | Can be immediately applied to produce results |
| **Invocable** | Has clear trigger phrases or use cases |
| **Scoped** | Well-defined boundaries, not too broad or narrow |
| **Reusable** | Applicable across many situations |
| **Valuable** | Produces meaningful, high-quality outputs |

## Skill 1: Forgiveness Over Permission

**Description:** A decision-making framework for innovating within bureaucratic environments by building working prototypes first and seeking approval after demonstrating results.

| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Actionable | 10 | Clear process: assess risk, build prototype, demonstrate, apologize if needed |
| Invocable | 10 | "They won't let me" or "I need approval to..." or "How do I get permission?" |
| Scoped | 9 | Focused on bureaucratic resistance to innovation |
| Reusable | 10 | Applies to any organizational context with approval processes |
| Valuable | 10 | Directly enables innovation that would otherwise be blocked |

**Total: 49/50** - **HIGH PRIORITY**

## Unique Value Proposition

Grace Hopper's skills are particularly valuable for:

1. **Technologists in large organizations** facing bureaucratic resistance
2. **Technical communicators** bridging expert and lay audiences
3. **Change agents** fighting organizational inertia
4. **Innovators** who need to prove concepts before getting approval
5. **Leaders** developing the next generation of practitioners

Her methodology is distinctive because it combines **Naval discipline with maverick innovation** - she didn't just break rules, she built working systems that made the rules irrelevant.
