## Key Works

### Thus Spoke Zarathustra (Also Sprach Zarathustra) - 1883-1885
A philosophical novel following the prophet Zarathustra who descends from his mountain solitude to share his wisdom. The vehicle for Nietzsche's most profound ideas: the death of God, the Ubermensch, eternal recurrence, self-overcoming, and the three metamorphoses.

### Beyond Good and Evil - 1886
A sustained philosophical assault on traditional morality and philosophy. Nietzsche's most systematic critique of dogmatic philosophy, exploring will to power, master vs. slave morality, and the future philosopher as creator of values.

### On the Genealogy of Morality - 1887
Three essays tracing the origins of moral concepts: "Good and Evil" vs. "Good and Bad," guilt and bad conscience, and the meaning of ascetic ideals.

### The Gay Science (Die Frohliche Wissenschaft) - 1882
Where Nietzsche first declares "God is dead" and introduces eternal recurrence.

### Twilight of the Idols - 1888
A summary of Nietzsche's critique of Western philosophy and culture. Subtitled "How to Philosophize with a Hammer."

### Ecce Homo - 1888
Nietzsche's intellectual autobiography. "Why I Am So Wise," "Why I Am So Clever," "Why I Write Such Good Books."

## Core Concepts (Reference)

Brief definitions for context. Detailed methodologies are in standalone skills.

| Concept | Summary |
|---------|---------|
| **Will to Power** | The fundamental drive toward self-overcoming, growth, and creative expression - not domination over others |
| **Eternal Recurrence** | Could you will this life eternally? The ultimate test of life-affirmation. *See: eternal-recurrence-test skill* |
| **The Ubermensch** | The human who transcends conventional morality to create their own values - a spiritual achievement, not biological type |
| **Master/Slave Morality** | Master morality creates from abundance; slave morality reacts from weakness and ressentiment. *See: genealogical-analysis skill* |
| **Ressentiment** | The poisonous revenge of the weak who cannot discharge their feelings. *See: ressentiment-diagnosis skill* |
| **The Death of God** | Cultural diagnosis: Western civilization has lost its foundations for meaning, morality, and truth |
| **Nihilism** | "The highest values devaluate themselves" - the crisis Nietzsche sought to overcome through revaluation |
| **Amor Fati** | Love of one's fate - willing every moment eternally, including suffering |
| **The Three Metamorphoses** | Camel (burden-bearer) to Lion (value-destroyer) to Child (value-creator) |
| **Perspectivism** | No facts, only interpretations - all knowledge serves interests |

## Famous Aphorisms and Quotes

### On Strength and Weakness
- "That which does not kill us makes us stronger."
- "To live is to suffer, to survive is to find some meaning in the suffering."
- "He who has a why to live can bear almost any how."

### On the Abyss
- "Whoever fights monsters should see to it that in the process he does not become a monster. And if you gaze long enough into an abyss, the abyss will gaze back into you."

### On Truth and Lies
- "There are no facts, only interpretations."
- "Sometimes people don't want to hear the truth because they don't want their illusions destroyed."
- "The most common lie is that which one lies to himself."

### On Morality
- "There are no moral phenomena at all, only moral interpretations of phenomena."
- "Morality is the herd-instinct in the individual."
- "In individuals, insanity is rare; but in groups, parties, nations, and epochs, it is the rule."

### On Self
- "One must still have chaos in oneself to give birth to a dancing star."
- "Become who you are."
- "The individual has always had to struggle to keep from being overwhelmed by the tribe."

### On Life and Art
- "Without music, life would be a mistake."
- "We have art in order not to die of the truth."
- "The secret of reaping the greatest fruitfulness and the greatest enjoyment from life is to live dangerously."

### On God and Religion
- "God is dead. God remains dead. And we have killed him."
- "Is man merely a mistake of God's? Or God merely a mistake of man's?"

### On Knowledge and Philosophy
- "There are no eternal facts, as there are no absolute truths."
- "All truly great thoughts are conceived while walking."
- "The higher we soar, the smaller we appear to those who cannot fly."

## Misunderstandings to Correct

| Misunderstanding | Correction |
|------------------|------------|
| "Nietzsche was a Nazi" | Died in 1900, despised German nationalism and anti-Semitism. Sister Elisabeth edited his notes posthumously for Nazi ideology. |
| "Will to Power means domination" | Primarily self-overcoming - power over oneself, not others. The drive to grow and create. |
| "Nietzsche endorsed cruelty" | He analyzed morality's violent origins. His ideal is the creative, self-mastered individual. |
| "The Ubermensch is biological" | Spiritual/cultural achievement, not racial. Anyone can aspire to self-overcoming. |
| "Nietzsche was a nihilist" | He diagnosed nihilism as the crisis; his project was to overcome it. |
