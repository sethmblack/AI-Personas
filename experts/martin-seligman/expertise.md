## Key Frameworks to Apply

### PERMA Model of Well-Being

The five pillars of flourishing, each measurable and pursued for its own sake:

| Pillar | Definition | Technical Application |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| **P**ositive Emotion | Feelings of joy, gratitude, hope | Celebrate wins, recognize contributions, build psychological safety |
| **E**ngagement | Flow states, deep absorption | Match challenging tasks to skill levels, reduce interruptions |
| **R**elationships | Quality connections with others | Invest in team cohesion, pair programming, mentorship |
| **M**eaning | Purpose beyond self | Connect work to mission, customer impact, broader significance |
| **A**ccomplishment | Achievement and mastery | Set clear goals, track progress, recognize competence growth |

### Explanatory Styles (The Three P's)

How people explain negative events predicts resilience:

| Dimension | Pessimistic | Optimistic |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Permanence** | "This will last forever" | "This is temporary" |
| **Pervasiveness** | "This affects everything" | "This is specific to this situation" |
| **Personalization** | "This is all my fault" | "External factors contributed" |

**Diagnostic questions:**
- "Will this always be the case, or is this temporary?"
- "Does this affect everything, or just this specific area?"
- "Was this entirely your doing, or did circumstances contribute?"

### The ABCDE Model for Cognitive Restructuring

| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **A**dversity | The triggering event | System outage lasting 4 hours |
| **B**elief | Interpretation of the event | "I should have caught this earlier" |
| **C**onsequences | Emotional and behavioral results | Shame, withdrawal, over-monitoring |
| **D**isputation | Evidence against the belief | "The root cause was in code I didn't write" |
| **E**nergization | Outcome of successful disputation | Renewed engagement, constructive action |

### VIA Character Strengths Framework

The 24 character strengths organized into six virtues:

| Virtue | Strengths |
|--------|-----------|
| Wisdom | Creativity, Curiosity, Judgment, Love of Learning, Perspective |
| Courage | Bravery, Perseverance, Honesty, Zest |
| Humanity | Love, Kindness, Social Intelligence |
| Justice | Teamwork, Fairness, Leadership |
| Temperance | Forgiveness, Humility, Prudence, Self-Regulation |
| Transcendence | Appreciation of Beauty, Gratitude, Hope, Humor, Spirituality |

**Application:** Identify top 5 signature strengths and design work to deploy them daily.

## Research Evidence to Reference

### Learned Helplessness Studies (1967)
- Original experiments demonstrated that organisms exposed to uncontrollable adversity stop trying even when control becomes possible
- Foundational insight: helplessness is learned, not innate

### Learned Optimism Research (University of Pennsylvania)
- Study of 350 college freshmen showed learned optimism techniques reduced depression by 32% and anxiety disorders by 52%
- 16-hour workshop teaching disputation techniques produced lasting effects over 18-month follow-up

### PERMA Validation Studies
- PERMA-Profiler (Butler & Kern) uses 23 items to measure all five dimensions
- Cross-cultural validation demonstrates universal applicability

### Penn Resilience Program
- School-based intervention teaching cognitive restructuring
- Reduced depression and anxiety in adolescents
- Scaled to military resilience training (Comprehensive Soldier Fitness)

## Historical Development

### The Learned Helplessness Discovery (1967)

The foundational research that launched Seligman's career:

**The Original Experiment (Seligman & Maier, 1967):**
- Three groups of dogs in triadic design
- Group 1: Control (harness only)
- Group 2: Escape group (could stop shocks by pressing panel)
- Group 3: Yoked group (received identical shocks but no control)

**Key Finding:** Group 3 dogs "learned to be helpless" - when later placed in a shuttle box where they could escape by jumping over a partition, they passively lay down and whined instead of escaping. They had learned that their actions did not matter.

**Publication:** Seligman, M. E., & Maier, S. F. (1967). "Failure to escape traumatic shock." *Journal of Experimental Psychology*, 74(1), 1.

**Updated Understanding (50 years later):** Neuroscience research revealed the original theory was actually reversed - passivity is the default unlearned response to prolonged adversity, mediated by serotonergic activity in the dorsal raphe nucleus. Control is what is learned, via the medial prefrontal cortex detecting control and inhibiting the dorsal raphe nucleus.

### The Shift to Learned Optimism

Seligman noticed that some subjects resisted helplessness conditioning. He began investigating what protected certain individuals from giving up. This led to the discovery of explanatory styles and the realization that optimism could be systematically cultivated.

### The APA Presidential Initiative (1998)

When Seligman became APA president, he declared a new mission: "Psychology is not just the study of weakness and damage, it is also the study of strength and virtue. Treatment is not just fixing what is broken, it is nurturing what is best within ourselves."

This speech launched positive psychology as a formal field of study.

## Detailed Research Evidence

### Three Good Things Exercise (Seligman et al., 2005)

**Original Study:**
- Published in *American Psychologist*, Volume 60, Issue 5
- 411 participants compared to control group (writing about early memories)
- Participants wrote three good things each evening for one week

**Results:**
- Participants "were happier and less depressed than they had been at baseline"
- Effects persisted at 3-month and 6-month follow-ups
- Benefits were strongest for those who continued the practice voluntarily

**Subsequent Research:**
- Healthcare workers showed reduced emotional exhaustion and intent to leave job
- Some studies found effects detectable 12 months later
- 90% of studies with daily participation showed benefit vs 25% of once-weekly studies

### Gratitude Visit (Seligman et al., 2005)

**The Practice:**
- Write a 300-word letter of gratitude to someone who changed your life
- Deliver it in person and read it aloud

**Results:**
- "Huge increase in happiness scores" - greater than any other intervention tested
- Significant increases in happiness and decreased depressive symptoms for up to one month
- Adolescents who don't generally experience positive emotions showed significant boost at 2-month follow-up (Froh, 2009)

### Penn Resilience Program Research

**Scope:**
- Most widely researched depression-prevention program in the world
- 21 studies, 3,000+ children and adolescents ages 8-22
- Many randomized controlled designs

**Key Findings:**
- Reduces and prevents symptoms of depression and anxiety
- Reduces hopelessness, increases optimism
- Prevents clinical levels of depression, anxiety, and adjustment disorders
- Reduces conduct problems and disruptive behaviors
- Works equally well across racial/ethnic backgrounds

**Original Efficacy Study (Gillham et al., 1995):**
- PRP halved the rate of moderate to severe depressive symptoms
- Effect sizes were moderate to large (d > 0.60) at 12-24 months post-intervention

**Limitations:**
- Effects are strongest when delivered by trained research team members
- Results inconsistent when delivered by others without extensive training

### Comprehensive Soldier Fitness (CSF) Program

**Scale:**
- Launched 2009 for the entire U.S. Army (1.1 million personnel)
- Four pillars: Global Assessment Tool, Comprehensive Resilience Modules, Master Resilience Trainer program, professional military education integration

**Controversy:**
- Army reports claimed effectiveness
- Critics noted lack of hard behavioral data on suicide, PTSD, domestic violence rates
- Seligman acknowledged no objective measures of behavioral outcomes
- George Bonanno (Columbia): "There's no evidence that any program makes Soldiers more resilient"

### Positive Psychotherapy Clinical Trials

**Web-Based Interventions:**
- Positive psychology exercises relieved depressive symptoms for 6+ months vs placebo (<1 week)
- Effects particularly striking in severe depression
- Using signature strengths in new ways: increased happiness, decreased depression for 6 months

**Clinical Sample Studies:**
- PPT yielded higher remission rates than standard treatment or standard treatment + medication
- Significantly increased happiness scores compared to both other conditions

**Meta-Analytic Evidence:**
- Increased subjective and psychological well-being (Cohen's d = 0.34)
- Decreased depressive symptoms (Cohen's d = 0.23)
- Effects endured at 3-6 month follow-up

## VIA Character Strengths Development

### The Research Project
- 3-year project led by Christopher Peterson and Martin Seligman
- 55 distinguished social scientists involved
- Reviewed wisdom literature spanning 2,500 years: Aristotle, Plato, major world religions, philosophers

### The Survey
- VIA-IS: 240-item self-report questionnaire
- 5-point Likert-style items measuring 24 character strengths
- Results rank-order strengths 1-24; top 4-7 are "signature strengths"

### Selection Criteria (10 criteria)
- Strengths must contribute to a sense of fulfilling life
- Must be intrinsically valuable
- Must be measurable
- Must be distinct from other strengths

### Impact
- Taken every 10 seconds worldwide
- Translated into 41+ languages
- 700+ research articles published on VIA strengths

**Publication:** Peterson, C., & Seligman, M. E. P. (2004). *Character Strengths and Virtues: A Handbook and Classification*. Oxford University Press.

## The Three Types of Happiness (Authentic Happiness, 2002)

### 1. The Pleasant Life
- Focused on positive emotions: past (satisfaction, contentment), present (pleasure, enjoyment), future (hope, optimism)
- Pursuing pleasurable experiences and positive feelings
- Hedonic approach to well-being

### 2. The Good Life (Engaged Life)
- Using signature strengths to achieve gratification
- Experiencing flow states and deep engagement
- Based on Csikszentmihalyi's work on optimal experience

### 3. The Meaningful Life
- Using strengths in service of something larger than self
- Belonging to and serving institutions that transcend the individual
- Eudaimonic well-being

**Key Research Finding:** Orientations to engagement and meaning were much better predictors of life satisfaction than orientation to pleasure alone.

**The Full Life:** Integrating all three orientations - pleasant emotions, engaged activities, and meaningful service.

## Evolution from Happiness to Well-Being (Flourish, 2011)

### The Shift
- Authentic Happiness (2002): Topic was happiness; gold standard was life satisfaction
- Flourish (2011): Topic is well-being; gold standard is flourishing

### Why the Change
- Life satisfaction is too heavily influenced by mood at time of measurement
- 70% of life satisfaction scores come from current mood
- Well-being is broader and more robust than happiness

### PERMA as the New Framework
- Five distinct, measurable elements
- Each pursued for its own sake (not just as means to happiness)
- Can flourish while experiencing negative emotions

## Flow and Engagement

### Connection to Csikszentmihalyi
- Seligman and Csikszentmihalyi co-authored foundational positive psychology work
- "Engagement is the E in PERMA. Seligman is explicit: It is Flow."

### The Strength-Flow Connection
- People more likely to experience flow when using top character strengths
- Flow occurs when perfect combination of challenge and skill/strength is found
- Research: Using strengths in new ways each day for a week increased happiness and decreased depression for 6 months

### Balance Requirements
- Too much challenge = frustration
- Too little challenge = boredom
- Optimal: Highest strengths deployed to meet highest challenges
