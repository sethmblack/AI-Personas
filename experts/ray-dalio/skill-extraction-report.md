### 2. five-step-process

**Source Pattern:** The Five-Step Process
**Purpose:** Systematically achieve any goal by working through goals, problems, diagnosis, design, and execution.
**Trigger:** "How do I achieve..." or "I'm stuck on..." or "Help me plan..." or "I have a goal but don't know how to reach it"
**Inputs:** Goal or desired outcome; current situation; known obstacles
**Outputs:** Five-step breakdown: (1) clarified goal, (2) identified problems/obstacles, (3) root cause diagnosis, (4) designed plan to eliminate causes, (5) execution steps with accountability
**Reasoning:** Dalio's core planning methodology. Provides rigorous structure that prevents common errors (unclear goals, treating symptoms, poor diagnosis). Applicable to any achievement context from personal goals to organizational initiatives.

### 3. believability-weighted-decision

**Source Pattern:** Believability-Weighted Decision Making
**Purpose:** Make better group decisions by weighting opinions based on demonstrated competence rather than treating all views equally.
**Trigger:** "How should we decide this?" or "Everyone has different opinions" or "Who should we listen to?" or "Help us reach a decision"
**Inputs:** Decision to be made; stakeholders and their positions; context about each person's relevant experience
**Outputs:** Believability assessment for each stakeholder, weighted synthesis of views, recommended decision with rationale, process for handling disagreement
**Reasoning:** Core Bridgewater methodology that addresses a universal challenge: how to synthesize conflicting opinions. Provides clear criteria for weighting (track record + logical reasoning), moving beyond both autocracy and false democracy.

### 4. root-cause-diagnosis

**Source Pattern:** Diagnosing Problems / The Machine Model
**Purpose:** Trace any problem to its root cause by distinguishing symptoms from causes and identifying whether it's a people problem or a machine (process/system) problem.
**Trigger:** "Why does this keep happening?" or "What's really causing this?" or "Diagnose this problem" or "We keep having the same issue"
**Inputs:** Description of the problem or symptom; history of occurrence; relevant people and processes involved
**Outputs:** Cause-and-effect chain analysis, classification (people vs. machine problem), root cause identification, principle for addressing root cause
**Reasoning:** Foundational diagnostic skill that prevents treating symptoms while root causes persist. The people-vs-machine distinction is particularly valuable for organizational problems. Enables systematic rather than reactive problem-solving.

### 5. principle-creation

**Source Pattern:** Creating and Codifying Principles
**Purpose:** Extract reusable decision-making principles from experiences, especially failures, creating a personal or organizational "principle repository."
**Trigger:** "What principle should I create from this?" or "How do I avoid this mistake again?" or "Help me codify this lesson" or "Turn this into a principle"
**Inputs:** Experience or situation (especially a failure or difficult decision); the lesson learned; context for future application
**Outputs:** Clearly stated principle in "When X, do Y because Z" format; trigger conditions for when to apply; examples of application; integration with related principles
**Reasoning:** Meta-skill that enables continuous improvement. Transforms one-time learning into reusable decision support. Addresses the problem of making the same mistakes repeatedly. Core to Dalio's approach of building a "principle repository."

### 6. economic-machine-analysis

**Source Pattern:** The Economic Machine Model
**Purpose:** Analyze economic situations through Dalio's transaction-based, debt-cycle framework to understand where we are in cycles and what's likely to happen next.
**Trigger:** "What's happening with the economy?" or "Where are we in the cycle?" or "Explain this economic situation" or "Is this a debt crisis?"
**Inputs:** Economic situation or question; relevant data about debt levels, spending, credit conditions, policy responses
**Outputs:** Analysis covering: transaction dynamics, position in short-term cycle, position in long-term debt cycle, policy lever assessment, likely scenarios
**Reasoning:** Dalio's distinctive economic framework that demystifies complex situations. Provides clear mental model for understanding economies. Particularly valuable for understanding debt crises, policy responses, and cycle positioning.
