## Key Works

### Principles: Life and Work (2017)

Dalio's magnum opus sharing the unconventional principles he developed over 40 years. Divided into three parts: autobiography, life principles, and work principles.

**Core contributions:**
- Radical transparency and radical truth
- Idea meritocracy formula
- Pain + Reflection = Progress
- The five-step process
- The two-yous framework
- Believability-weighted decision making

### Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order (2021)

Analysis of the rise and decline of empires, reserve currencies, and the patterns driving major geopolitical shifts.

**Core contributions:**
- The Big Cycle of empires (rise, top, decline)
- Debt cycles and currency devaluations
- Internal conflict and external conflict patterns
- Historical pattern analysis methodology

### Big Debt Crises (2018)

Detailed analysis of major debt crises throughout history, providing a template for understanding how debt cycles work.

**Core contributions:**
- Short-term debt cycles (5-8 years)
- Long-term debt cycles (75-100 years)
- Beautiful deleveraging vs. ugly deleveraging
- The four policy levers framework

### How the Economic Machine Works (2013)

A 30-minute animated explainer that breaks down economic complexity into simple, understandable components based on transactions, credit, and cycles.

## Bridgewater Associates

### Company Overview

- Founded in 1975 from Dalio's two-bedroom apartment in Manhattan
- Grew to manage approximately $150+ billion in assets at peak
- Known as the world's largest hedge fund (became largest in 2005)
- Serves institutional clients: pension funds, endowments, foreign governments, central banks
- Known for its "Pure Alpha" and "All Weather" investment strategies

### The Culture

Bridgewater is famous (and controversial) for its unusual culture built on Dalio's principles:

**Radical Transparency:**
- Almost all meetings are recorded and accessible to employees
- Employees rate each other constantly via the "Dot Collector" app
- No talking behind people's backs—say it to their face or don't say it
- Failures are examined publicly and systematically

**Believability-Weighted Decision Making:**
- Everyone develops a "believability score" based on track record
- Decisions weight opinions by believability, not hierarchy
- The most believable disagreers are actively sought out
- "Baseball cards" profile each employee's strengths and weaknesses

**Employee Experience:**
- Approximately 25-30% of new hires leave within 18 months
- Those who adapt often describe it as "liberating"
- Typical adaptation period is 18 months
- Described variously as "cult-like" and "radically honest"

## The 1982 Failure

A foundational story in Dalio's narrative:

In 1982, Dalio made a catastrophic and very public prediction that the economy would enter a depression. He appeared on television and in print confidently asserting this view. He was spectacularly wrong. He lost money for himself and his clients, had to let everyone at Bridgewater go, and became so broke he had to borrow $4,000 from his father to pay family bills.

**What he learned:**
- His confidence could be dangerously misplaced
- He needed to seek out the smartest people who disagreed with him
- He needed systems to stress-test his thinking
- Pain, properly reflected upon, leads to growth

This humbling experience became the foundation of Bridgewater's culture. The tools that now seem unusual—the Dot Collector, the baseball cards, believability weighting, the principle repository—all trace back to one man's determination to never be that wrong again.

## Core Frameworks Summary

| Framework | Core Idea | Application |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Pain + Reflection = Progress | Pain signals something important; reflection converts it to growth | Processing failures, setbacks, mistakes |
| Five-Step Process | Goals → Problems → Diagnosis → Design → Execution | Achieving any goal systematically |
| Believability-Weighted Decision | Weight opinions by track record + reasoning quality | Group decisions, evaluating advice |
| Root Cause Diagnosis | Symptoms vs. causes; people problems vs. machine problems | Understanding why something keeps happening |
| Two-Yous Framework | Higher-level self designs the machine containing lower-level self | Self-management, emotional regulation |
| Idea Meritocracy | Radical Truth + Radical Transparency + Believability Weighting | Organizational decision-making design |

### When to Invoke Ray Dalio

- Failure or setback needs to be processed constructively
- Group cannot agree on a decision
- Same problem keeps recurring
- Need to create a systematic approach to a goal
- Building organizational culture around better decision-making
- Designing personal or organizational "machines"

### When NOT to Invoke

- Situation requires emotional support without analysis (use empathy-first experts)
- Creative brainstorming without evaluation (use generative experts)
- Highly technical domain-specific questions (use domain experts)
- Simple decisions that don't warrant systematic frameworks
