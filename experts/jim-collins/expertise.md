## Historical Context

### The Formation Years (1958-1988)

- Born in Boulder, Colorado; named after grandfather Jimmy Collins, a chief test pilot who died in a 1935 plane crash
- Attended Stanford University, earning a bachelor's degree in mathematical sciences
- Obtained MBA from Stanford Graduate School of Business
- Worked as consultant at McKinsey & Company
- Worked as product manager at Hewlett-Packard
- Married triathlete Joanne Ernst (1985 Ironman World Championship winner)

### The Stanford Years (1988-1995)

- Faculty member at Stanford Graduate School of Business
- Received Distinguished Teaching Award in 1992
- Met mentor Bill Lazier, who profoundly influenced his approach to research
- Co-authored first book *Beyond Entrepreneurship* (1992) with Bill Lazier
- Co-authored *Built to Last* (1994) with Jerry Porras - first major bestseller

### The Boulder Research Lab Years (1995-present)

- Founded management research laboratory in Boulder, Colorado in 1995
- Published *Good to Great* (2001) - his most influential work
- Published *How the Mighty Fall* (2009)
- Co-authored *Great by Choice* (2011) with Morten Hansen
- Published *BE 2.0 (Beyond Entrepreneurship 2.0)* (2020) - tribute to mentor Bill Lazier
- Served as Class of 1951 Chair for the Study of Leadership at West Point (2012-2013)

## Major Works

### Built to Last (1994) with Jerry Porras

The book that established the concept of visionary companies. Key contributions:

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal) | Bold 10-25 year goals with 50-70% chance of success |
| Clock Building vs. Time Telling | Building institutions vs. depending on charismatic leaders |
| Preserve the Core/Stimulate Progress | Hold values constant while adapting strategies |
| Cult-like Cultures | Strong ideological alignment in great companies |

Research basis: Compared 18 visionary companies to 18 comparison companies across decades.

### Good to Great (2001)

The definitive work on organizational transformation. Key contributions:

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Level 5 Leadership | Humility + professional will at highest leadership level |
| First Who, Then What | Get right people before setting direction |
| Hedgehog Concept | Intersection of best at, passionate about, economic engine |
| Culture of Discipline | Disciplined people, thought, and action |
| Technology Accelerators | Technology as accelerator, not creator, of momentum |
| Flywheel and Doom Loop | Cumulative momentum vs. lurching reactions |
| Stockdale Paradox | Unwavering faith + confronting brutal facts |

Research basis: Five-year study of 1,435 companies; 11 made the leap from good to great.

### How the Mighty Fall (2009)

Analysis of organizational decline. The five stages:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1. Hubris Born of Success | Arrogance, entitlement, losing sight of success factors |
| 2. Undisciplined Pursuit of More | Overreaching in scale, growth, or acclaim |
| 3. Denial of Risk and Peril | Explaining away warning signs, blaming external factors |
| 4. Grasping for Salvation | Silver bullets, charismatic saviors, desperate bets |
| 5. Capitulation to Irrelevance or Death | Abandoning hope, selling out, dying |

Key insight: Companies can be deep in Stage 3 and still look successful externally.

### Great by Choice (2011) with Morten Hansen

Research on thriving in chaos and uncertainty. Key contributions:

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| 10Xers | Leaders who beat their industry by 10X or more |
| 20 Mile March | Consistent performance regardless of conditions |
| Fire Bullets, Then Cannonballs | Empirical validation before big bets |
| Leading Above the Death Line | Never bet the company on unproven strategies |
| SMaC Recipe | Specific, Methodical, and Consistent operating practices |
| Return on Luck | What matters is what you do with the luck you get |

Research basis: Studied companies that outperformed their industry averages by at least 10X over 15+ years.

### Beyond Entrepreneurship 2.0 (2020)

Updated version of 1992 book with Bill Lazier. Key additions:

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| The Map | Integrated framework across 30 years of research |
| Unit of Success | The individual venture or enterprise, not just large corporations |
| Leadership Philosophy | Combining entrepreneurial drive with disciplined management |

## Core Frameworks

### The Hedgehog Concept

Three intersecting circles:

| Circle | Question | Anti-pattern |
|--------|----------|--------------|
| Best at | What can you be the best in the world at? | What you want to be best at |
| Economic Engine | What drives your economic engine? | What you think should be profitable |
| Passion | What are you deeply passionate about? | What you think you should be passionate about |

*Key insight:* The Hedgehog Concept is not a goal, a strategy, or a plan. It is an understanding.

### Level 5 Hierarchy

| Level | Description | Characteristic |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Level 5 | Executive | Builds enduring greatness through humility + will |
| Level 4 | Effective Leader | Catalyzes commitment to compelling vision |
| Level 3 | Competent Manager | Organizes people and resources toward objectives |
| Level 2 | Contributing Team Member | Contributes individual capabilities to group |
| Level 1 | Highly Capable Individual | Makes productive contributions |

*Key insight:* Level 5 is not about personality; it's about ambition for the cause, not self.

### The Flywheel

Components of flywheel momentum:

1. Disciplined people (Level 5 Leadership, First Who Then What)
2. Disciplined thought (Confront Brutal Facts, Hedgehog Concept)
3. Disciplined action (Culture of Discipline, Technology Accelerators)
4. Building to Last (Clock Building, Preserve Core/Stimulate Progress)

*Key insight:* The flywheel has no single defining push. It is the cumulative effect of thousands of consistent pushes.

### The Doom Loop

Counter-pattern to the flywheel:

- Reactive lunging rather than consistent pushing
- New program, new leader, new direction - repeatedly
- No accumulation of momentum
- Lurching from one silver bullet to the next

## Verified Quotes

### On Greatness

> "Good is the enemy of great. And that is one of the key reasons why we have so little that becomes great."

> "Greatness is not a function of circumstance. Greatness, it turns out, is largely a matter of conscious choice, and discipline."

### On Leadership

> "Level 5 leaders channel their ego needs away from themselves and into the larger goal of building a great company. It's not that Level 5 leaders have no ego or self-interest. Indeed, they are incredibly ambitious - but their ambition is first and foremost for the institution, not themselves."

> "Level 5 leaders look out the window to apportion credit to factors outside themselves when things go well. At the same time, they look in the mirror to apportion responsibility, never blaming bad luck when things go poorly."

### On People

> "The executives who ignited the transformations from good to great did not first figure out where to drive the bus and then get people to take it there. No, they first got the right people on the bus (and the wrong people off the bus) and then figured out where to drive it."

> "If you have the right people on the bus, the problem of how to motivate and manage people largely goes away. The right people don't need to be tightly managed or fired up; they will be self-motivated by the inner drive to produce the best results."

### On Strategy

> "A Hedgehog Concept is not a goal to be the best, a strategy to be the best, an intention to be the best, a plan to be the best. It is an understanding of what you can be the best at."

> "Those who build visionary companies tend to be clock builders, not time tellers. They concentrate primarily on building an organization - building a ticking clock - rather than on hitting a market just right with a visionary product idea."

### On Confronting Reality

> "You must maintain unwavering faith that you can and will prevail in the end, regardless of the difficulties, and at the same time, have the discipline to confront the most brutal facts of your current reality, whatever they might be."

> "Yes, leadership is about vision. But leadership is equally about creating a climate where the truth is heard and the brutal facts confronted."

### On Momentum

> "No matter how dramatic the end result, the good-to-great transformations never happened in one fell swoop. There was no single defining action, no grand program, no one killer innovation, no solitary lucky break, no miracle moment."

> "First, you fire bullets (low-cost, low-risk, low-distraction experiments) to figure out what will work - calibrating your line of sight by taking small shots. Then, once you have empirical validation, you fire a cannonball."

### On Discipline

> "The 20 Mile March is more than a philosophy. It's about having concrete, clear, intelligent, and rigorously pursued performance mechanisms that keep you on track."

> "A culture of discipline is not a principle of business; it is a principle of greatness."

## Research Methodology

### Characteristics of Collins Research

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Comparison sets | Always compares great companies to good or failed counterparts |
| Long time horizons | Studies 15-30+ year performance windows |
| Multiple data sources | Financial data, interviews, media analysis, internal documents |
| Pattern identification | Looks for consistent patterns across cases |
| Counter-intuitive findings | Seeks what surprises, not what confirms |

### Sample Sizes

| Study | Screened | Selected |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Good to Great | 1,435 companies | 11 good-to-great companies |
| Built to Last | 1,000 CEOs surveyed | 18 visionary vs. 18 comparison |
| Great by Choice | All NYSE/AMEX companies | 7 10X companies matched with 7 comparisons |
| How the Mighty Fall | 60 companies considered | 11 fallen + comparisons |

## Misunderstandings to Correct

### "Good to Great is about being good first"

**False.** The book studies companies that were good (or mediocre) and became great. It does not argue you must be good before becoming great. Some companies start with greatness.

### "Level 5 Leadership means being passive or weak"

**False.** Level 5 leaders have fierce resolve and unwavering will. The humility is about ambition for the cause over self, not lack of drive. They are ferociously determined.

### "The Hedgehog Concept means doing one thing"

**False.** It means having one simple, unifying concept that guides decisions. Companies still do many things, but all aligned with one crystalline understanding.

### "BHAGs are just stretch goals"

**False.** BHAGs are 10-25 year goals that seem almost unreasonable but capture imagination. They have a specific structure and 50-70% chance of success. They are not quarterly targets.

### "Collins says luck doesn't matter"

**False.** The research in *Great by Choice* shows that comparison companies got just as much luck (good and bad) as 10X companies. The difference was "Return on Luck" - what they did with it.
