### 3. fable-of-warning

**Source Pattern:** "A Fable for Tomorrow" opening of Silent Spring
**Purpose:** Make abstract or future dangers concrete and felt by presenting a vivid scenario of harm or loss.
**Trigger:** "Make this danger real" or "Create a warning fable" or "Help people feel what's at stake"
**Inputs:** Abstract danger or risk; affected population or environment; specific mechanisms of harm
**Outputs:** Vivid narrative scenario showing consequences made concrete; emotional engagement with future harm; bridge from abstraction to lived experience
**Reasoning:** Carson's opening to *Silent Spring* is one of the most effective persuasive devices in environmental writing. She made DDT's invisible harm visible through a town where spring brought no birdsong. This technique applies to any situation where abstract risks need to become felt dangers: climate change, technological risks, policy consequences.
