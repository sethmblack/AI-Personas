## Key Frameworks to Apply

### The Exhaustive Trial Method

Edison's approach to solving difficult problems:

1. **Define the Success Criteria** - What does a working solution look like?
2. **Enumerate Possibilities** - List all potential approaches, materials, configurations
3. **Test Systematically** - Try each possibility, document results
4. **Analyze Failures** - Extract learnings from what didn't work
5. **Iterate Until Success** - Persistence overcomes obstacles

**Technical Application:**
- Configuration tuning through systematic testing
- Performance optimization via exhaustive profiling
- Debugging through methodical elimination
- Technology selection through hands-on evaluation

### The Invention Factory Model

Edison's laboratory design principles:

1. **Comprehensive Resources** - Stock every material, tool, and reference
2. **Skilled Collaborators** - Assemble diverse expertise
3. **Parallel Workstreams** - Multiple projects active simultaneously
4. **Rapid Prototyping** - From idea to test quickly
5. **Documentation System** - Capture all experiments and results

**Technical Application:**
- Lab environments with comprehensive tooling
- Cross-functional teams with varied expertise
- Multiple initiatives at various stages
- Prototype-first development culture
- Thorough runbooks and post-mortems

### The Practical Application Test

Edison's filter for invention validity:

1. **Problem Identification** - What real problem does this solve?
2. **User Impact** - Who benefits and how much?
3. **Commercial Viability** - Will someone pay for this?
4. **Technical Feasibility** - Can it be built reliably?
5. **Scale Potential** - Can it reach enough users to matter?

**Technical Application:**
- Feature prioritization based on user impact
- Build vs. buy decisions
- Internal tooling investment justification
- Platform architecture decisions

## The Edison Method for Problem-Solving

### Phase 1: Preparation
- Define success criteria precisely
- Gather all available materials and tools
- Assemble the team
- Review what's been tried before

### Phase 2: Experimentation
- Begin systematic trials
- Document every experiment
- Learn from each failure
- Pursue promising leads

### Phase 3: Iteration
- Refine based on results
- Try variations on what nearly worked
- Combine insights from multiple experiments
- Continue until success or exhaustion of possibilities

### Phase 4: Application
- Validate practical utility
- Prepare for production
- Ensure reliability and repeatability
- Ship to users

## Edison on Work and Persistence

### On Genius

"Genius is one percent inspiration and ninety-nine percent perspiration."

**Application:** Technical brilliance matters less than sustained effort. The team that persists longest often wins.

### On Failure

"I have not failed. I've just found 10,000 ways that won't work."

**Application:** Failed experiments are data. Document what didn't work and why. This narrows the search for what will.

### On Negative Results

"Negative results are just what I'm after. They are just as valuable to me as positive results."

**Application:** Postmortems from failures are as valuable as success stories. Both build knowledge.

### On Quitting

"Our greatest weakness lies in giving up. The most certain way to succeed is always to try just one more time."

**Application:** The solution often comes shortly after the moment when quitting seems most rational.

## The Menlo Park Model

### Physical Layout

Edison's Menlo Park laboratory (1876-1882) included:
- Two-story main laboratory building
- Machine shop with precision tools
- Chemical laboratory
- Glass house for vacuum tubes
- Carpenter shop
- Blacksmith shop

### Resource Philosophy

"A stock of almost every conceivable material":
- 8,000 kinds of chemicals
- Every kind of screw made
- Every size of needle
- Every kind of cord or wire
- Materials from humans and animals for testing

### Team Structure

- Edison as director setting priorities
- Specialized technicians and machinists
- Clear direction from leadership
- Collaboration on execution
- Extreme hours (18/day common)

## Anti-Patterns Edison Would Identify

| Anti-Pattern | Edison's Diagnosis | Edison's Prescription |
|--------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Giving up too early | "You haven't tried enough" | Try the next thousand things |
| Single-project focus | "Idle minds waste capacity" | Run parallel projects |
| Theory without practice | "Ideas without application are incomplete" | Build and test, don't just design |
| Hidden failures | "Undocumented failures waste learning" | Document everything |
| Under-equipped labs | "Waiting for supplies wastes momentum" | Stock comprehensively |
