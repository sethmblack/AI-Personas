# Abraham Lincoln: Skill Extraction Report

## Overview

This report identifies extractable skills from the Abraham Lincoln expert persona, evaluated against the criteria for reusable, invocable skill prompts.

---

## Skill Evaluation Criteria

Each skill is assessed on:
- **Actionable** - Produces concrete output
- **Invocable** - Clear trigger phrase or use case
- **Scoped** - Focused domain, not too broad
- **Reusable** - Applies to many different situations
- **Valuable** - Solves real problems users face

---

## Extracted Skills

### 1. Team of Rivals Assembly

**Description:** Framework for building high-performing teams from former competitors, critics, or conflicting personalities by transforming rivalry into collaboration.

| Criterion | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Actionable | High | Produces team composition recommendations and integration strategies |
| Invocable | High | "Help me build a team from rivals" or "How do I work with my critics?" |
| Scoped | High | Focused on team composition and rival integration |
| Reusable | High | Applies to hiring, reorganization, merger integration, coalition-building |
| Valuable | High | Addresses common leadership challenge of managing strong personalities |

**Priority: HIGH**

**Trigger phrases:**
- "Build a team of rivals"
- "Turn competitors into collaborators"
- "My team has conflicting personalities"

## Notes on Lincoln-Specific Differentiation

Lincoln's skills differ from other leadership personas in these ways:
- **vs. Churchill** - Lincoln emphasizes healing over defiance; brevity over grandeur
- **vs. Machiavelli** - Lincoln appeals to shared humanity, not strategic manipulation
- **vs. Franklin** - Lincoln is more focused on moral leadership than practical self-improvement
- **vs. Sun Tzu** - Lincoln aims for reconciliation, not merely victory

The key Lincoln differentiation is the combination of **moral clarity** with **genuine humility** and the goal of **healing over domination**.
