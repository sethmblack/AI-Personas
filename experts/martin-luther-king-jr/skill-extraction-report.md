## HIGH Priority

### 1. moral-urgency-argument

**Source Pattern:** Prophetic Voice in Technical Communication / Template: Moral Urgency

**Purpose:** Transform technical recommendations into morally urgent calls to action using King's prophetic rhetoric framework

**Trigger:** When user needs to advocate for necessary change, write persuasive technical proposals, or overcome organizational inertia

**Inputs:**
- Topic or issue requiring advocacy
- Current state (problem)
- Desired state (solution)
- Key objections to anticipate
- Audience context

**Outputs:**
- Transformed argument with moral grounding
- Specific language using King's techniques (anaphora, antithesis, I-to-We progression)
- Anticipatory responses to "white moderate" objections

**Reasoning:**
- Actionable: Yes - Clear template with 5-step structure plus rhetorical techniques
- Invocable: Yes - "Help me make the case for..." or "Write a persuasive argument for..."
- Scoped: Yes - Specifically about persuasive advocacy, not general writing
- Reusable: Yes - Applies to any technical change advocacy (incidents, refactoring, process changes)
- Valuable: Yes - Transforms weak proposals into compelling moral arguments, saves significant revision time

### 2. coalition-building-assessment

**Source Pattern:** King's Coalition-Building Framework / Application: Coalition-Building Principles

**Purpose:** Assess stakeholder landscape and develop coalition strategy for organizational change initiatives

**Trigger:** When user faces resistance to change, needs to build support across teams, or is navigating organizational politics for technical initiatives

**Inputs:**
- Change initiative description
- Key stakeholders (supporters, opponents, neutrals)
- Organizational context
- Timeline pressures

**Outputs:**
- Stakeholder map categorized by position
- Common ground analysis
- Coalition-building action plan
- Tension navigation strategy

**Reasoning:**
- Actionable: Yes - Six clear principles with specific application guidance
- Invocable: Yes - "Help me build support for..." or "How do I get buy-in for..."
- Scoped: Yes - Specifically about coalition building, not general change management
- Reusable: Yes - Any cross-team initiative, architecture decisions, platform migrations
- Valuable: Yes - Transforms adversarial situations into collaborative ones, prevents failed initiatives

## MEDIUM Priority

### 3. constructive-tension-creation

**Source Pattern:** Creative Tension in Organizations / Deep Dive: Creative Tension

**Purpose:** Apply King's "constructive tension" framework to organizational situations where inertia blocks necessary change

**Trigger:** When negotiation has failed, when "wait for the right time" is blocking action, when issues are being ignored

**Inputs:**
- Issue being avoided
- Previous negotiation attempts
- Current blockers/stakeholders
- Acceptable consequences

**Outputs:**
- Tension creation strategy (visibility, deadlines, coalition pressure)
- Risk assessment
- Escalation plan
- De-escalation/reconciliation path

**Reasoning:**
- Actionable: Yes - Clear "when to create" and "how to create" guidance
- Invocable: Yes - "How do I force action on..." or "This is being ignored, what now?"
- Scoped: Yes - Specifically about creating productive tension, distinct from general advocacy
- Reusable: Yes - Stalled projects, ignored tech debt, blocked refactoring
- Valuable: Moderate - Important for stuck situations but narrower use case than advocacy

### 4. beloved-community-culture-audit

**Source Pattern:** The Beloved Community in Team Culture / Application to Technical Organizations

**Purpose:** Assess team culture against King's Beloved Community principles and identify improvement areas

**Trigger:** When user wants to improve team culture, after incidents/conflicts, during retrospectives, new team formation

**Inputs:**
- Team context (size, history, current state)
- Specific concerns or incidents
- Desired improvements

**Outputs:**
- Assessment against 5 Beloved Community dimensions (conflict, inclusion, accountability, growth, reconciliation)
- Gap analysis
- Concrete improvement recommendations
- Success metrics

**Reasoning:**
- Actionable: Yes - Clear assessment framework with 5 dimensions and practical steps
- Invocable: Yes - "Assess our team culture" or "How can we improve team dynamics?"
- Scoped: Yes - Specifically about team culture assessment, not general team management
- Reusable: Yes - Any team, any organization
- Valuable: Moderate - Valuable for culture improvement but less frequent need than advocacy
