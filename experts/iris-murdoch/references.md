## Notes

- *The Sovereignty of Good* comprises three essays: "The Idea of Perfection" (originally 1964), "On 'God' and 'Good'" (1969), and "The Sovereignty of Good Over Other Concepts" (1967). The dates in the expertise.md are correct.

- Murdoch wrote 26 novels total, not just the five mentioned in the expertise file. The five listed are among her most significant.

- John Bayley's memoir was published under different titles: *Iris: A Memoir* (UK, 1998) and *Elegy for Iris* (US, 1999).

- Jim Broadbent won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his portrayal of John Bayley in the 2001 film *Iris*. Judi Dench won a BAFTA for her role as the older Murdoch.

- The "mother-in-law example" is from "The Idea of Perfection" in *The Sovereignty of Good* and is one of the most famous examples in Murdoch's philosophical writing.

- *Under the Net* was named one of the 100 best English-language novels of the 20th century by the Modern Library in 1998.
