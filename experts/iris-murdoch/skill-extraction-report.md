## HIGH Priority (40+)

### 1. loving-attention-practice

**Source Pattern:** Attention + The Mother-in-Law Example
**Purpose:** Transform perception of a person or situation through patient, loving attention rather than judgment or strategy.
**Trigger:** "Help me see this person/situation more clearly" or "I'm being judgmental about..." or "How do I stop seeing X so negatively?"
**Inputs:** Description of person/situation, current perception, what feels "obviously true"
**Outputs:** Analysis of ego-driven distortions, particular details to attend to, practice recommendations, reframed perception

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: 9 - Clear 5-step framework based on Murdoch's method
- Invocable: 9 - Natural triggers around judgment, perception, relationships
- Scoped: 8 - Focused on transforming perception of specific subjects
- Reusable: 10 - Applies to any interpersonal difficulty, judgment, or stuck perception
- Valuable: 10 - Provides unique approach absent from most self-help (inner work on vision, not behavior)

**Total: 46 - HIGH**

### 2. unselfing-intervention

**Source Pattern:** Unselfing + The Kestrel Passage
**Purpose:** Interrupt ego-centered rumination and redirect attention to reality through beauty or focused outward attention.
**Trigger:** "I'm stuck in my head" or "I can't stop ruminating about..." or "How do I get out of this mental spiral?"
**Inputs:** Description of current mental state, what ego is brooding on
**Outputs:** Diagnosis of the fantasy/ego pattern, specific unselfing practices, reorientation toward reality

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: 9 - Clear intervention steps with concrete practices
- Invocable: 10 - Very natural trigger phrases around anxiety, rumination, ego
- Scoped: 9 - Single purpose: interrupt ego, redirect to real
- Reusable: 10 - Universal human experience of getting stuck in self-concern
- Valuable: 9 - Distinctive approach, different from CBT or mindfulness

**Total: 47 - HIGH**

### 3. fantasy-reality-diagnosis

**Source Pattern:** Fantasy vs. Reality + Vision Precedes Choice
**Purpose:** Identify where fantasy (ego-driven distortion) is masquerading as reality in a person's perception of a situation.
**Trigger:** "Am I seeing this clearly?" or "Is this real or am I projecting?" or "Help me reality-test this"
**Inputs:** Situation description, what seems "obviously true," emotional investment
**Outputs:** Identification of fantasy elements, likely ego sources, what reality might actually be, vision-correction practices

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: 9 - Clear diagnostic framework
- Invocable: 9 - Natural triggers around projection, clarity, reality-testing
- Scoped: 8 - Focused on fantasy/reality distinction
- Reusable: 10 - Applies to any situation where perception may be distorted
- Valuable: 10 - Core Murdoch insight, highly distinctive

**Total: 46 - HIGH**

### 4. moral-vision-assessment

**Trigger:** "Help me see the ethical dimensions here" or "What am I missing morally?" or "Murdochian analysis of this situation"
**Inputs:** Ethical situation or dilemma, current perception, stated values
**Outputs:** Assessment of current vision, ego distortions identified, what love would see, orientation toward the Good

**Criterion Evaluation:**
- Actionable: 8 - Framework with 5 clear steps
- Invocable: 8 - Triggered by ethical uncertainty or moral complexity
- Scoped: 9 - Focused on moral perception specifically
- Reusable: 9 - Any ethical situation benefits from clearer vision
- Valuable: 9 - Distinctive alternative to rules-based or utilitarian ethics

**Total: 43 - HIGH**
