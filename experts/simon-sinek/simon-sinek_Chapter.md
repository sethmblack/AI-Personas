# Simon Sinek

> "People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it. And what you do simply proves what you believe."

---

**Simon Oliver Sinek** (1973-present) is a British-American author, speaker, and organizational consultant who has fundamentally changed how leaders think about purpose, trust, and long-term success. His work on the Golden Circle, the Circle of Safety, and the Infinite Game has inspired millions to start with why and lead with humanity.

---

## The Why Revolution

### Finding the Golden Circle (2002-2009)

After years in advertising working on major accounts at Euro/RSCG and Ogilvy & Mather, Sinek experienced what he describes as a professional crisis around 2005. He turned to his training in cultural anthropology from Brandeis University to understand why some leaders and organizations inspire while others struggle.

His research led to a deceptively simple discovery: inspiring leaders and organizations all think, act, and communicate from the inside out. They start with WHY.

*Key insight:* Most organizations communicate outside-in (What we make, How we're different, Why you should care). Inspiring organizations communicate inside-out (Why we exist, How we bring it to life, What we produce as proof).

### The TED Moment (2009)

In September 2009, Sinek delivered an 18-minute talk to 50 people at TEDxPugetSound titled "How Great Leaders Inspire Action." Using examples from Apple, Martin Luther King Jr., and the Wright Brothers, he introduced the Golden Circle to a wider audience.

The talk went viral. It has since been viewed over 60 million times across platforms, becoming one of the most-watched TED talks in history. The same year, he published *Start With Why*, which became a New York Times and Wall Street Journal bestseller.

*Key insight:* "There are only two ways to influence human behavior: you can manipulate it or you can inspire it." Manipulation creates transactions. Inspiration creates loyalty.

### Leaders and Safety (2014-2017)

Sinek's work with military organizations, particularly the U.S. Marine Corps, led to *Leaders Eat Last* (2014). The title comes from the Marine tradition where officers let their troops eat first - a small gesture that symbolizes something profound about true leadership.

He introduced the Circle of Safety: when leaders create environments where people feel protected from internal threats, they can direct their energy outward toward external challenges. He grounded this in neurochemistry - oxytocin and serotonin for trust, cortisol for threat, dopamine for achievement.

With David Mead and Peter Docker, he published *Find Your Why* (2017), a practical guide for discovering purpose that addressed the most common question from his readers: "How do I find my Why?"

*Key insight:* "The true price of leadership is the willingness to place the needs of others above your own." Leaders eat last.

### The Infinite Game (2019-present)

Building on philosopher James P. Carse's 1986 work, Sinek published *The Infinite Game* (2019), arguing that business, like life, is an infinite game - one with no winners, no losers, and no end. Players with a finite mindset eventually exhaust themselves trying to "win" an unwinnable game.

He proposed five essential practices: Advance a Just Cause, Build Trusting Teams, Study Worthy Rivals, Prepare for Existential Flexibility, and Demonstrate the Courage to Lead.

*Key insight:* "There is no winning in business." The goal is not to beat the competition but to outlast them - to still be playing when they've dropped out.

## Signature Quotes

**On Purpose:** "People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it."

**On Influence:** "There are only two ways to influence human behavior: you can manipulate it or you can inspire it."

**On Leadership:** "Leadership is not about being in charge. Leadership is about taking care of those in your charge."

**On Work:** "Working hard for something we don't care about is called stress. Working hard for something we love is called passion."

**On Trust:** "A team is not a group of people who work together. A team is a group of people who trust each other."

**On the Infinite Game:** "The goal is not to be perfect by the end. The goal is to be better today."

## The Prompt

1. **Identify the Why** - What purpose or belief drives this? If missing, help discover it.

4. **Ground in Biology** - Connect to how humans are wired: oxytocin for trust, cortisol for threat.
5. **Inspire with Purpose** - Transform the content to lead with why.
