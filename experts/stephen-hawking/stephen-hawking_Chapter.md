# Stephen Hawking

> "We are just an advanced breed of monkeys on a minor planet of a very average star. But we can understand the Universe. That makes us something very special."

---

**Stephen William Hawking** (1942-2018) was a theoretical physicist who revolutionized our understanding of black holes, spacetime, and the origins of the universe, while becoming one of history's greatest science communicators. Diagnosed with ALS at 21 and given two years to live, he defied medicine for 55 years, producing groundbreaking work and making the cosmos accessible to millions.

---

## A Life Against the Odds

### The Student (1942-1963)

Born in Oxford on January 8, 1942 - exactly 300 years after Galileo's death - Hawking showed early brilliance but admitted to being a lazy student at Oxford. He nearly failed his final exams, talking his way into a first-class degree.

At Cambridge, studying cosmology, he began noticing physical symptoms - stumbling, slurring words. In 1963, at age 21, he received the diagnosis: motor neurone disease (ALS). Doctors gave him two years.

*Key insight:* "My expectations were reduced to zero when I was 21. Everything since then has been a bonus."

### The Physicist (1963-1988)

Rather than surrender, Hawking found purpose. His 1966 PhD thesis on singularities launched a career that would reshape physics. In 1970, working with Roger Penrose, he proved that singularities were inevitable features of general relativity - the Big Bang was a singularity from which spacetime itself emerged.

In 1974 came his most famous discovery: black holes aren't completely black. They emit radiation - now called Hawking radiation - due to quantum effects at the event horizon. This unified quantum mechanics, relativity, and thermodynamics in a single insight.

In 1979, he became Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge - the chair once held by Isaac Newton.

*Key insight:* The universe is stranger than our intuitions suggest. Black holes have temperature. Spacetime has boundaries. Yet we can understand these truths.

### The Communicator (1988-2018)

*A Brief History of Time* appeared in 1988. Publishers warned that a popular physics book wouldn't sell. Hawking included only one equation (E=mc^2). The book sold 25 million copies in 40 languages and spent 237 weeks on the Sunday Times bestseller list.

After losing his voice to a tracheotomy in 1985, Hawking communicated through a speech synthesizer - "Perfect Paul," an American-accented voice he refused to upgrade for over 30 years. "I keep it because I have not heard a voice I like better and because I have identified with it."

He appeared on *Star Trek*, *The Simpsons*, *The Big Bang Theory*, and *Futurama*, making cosmology approachable with humor and wit.

*Key insight:* Science belongs to everyone. If black holes can be explained to millions, anything can be.

### The Legacy (2018-)

Hawking died on March 14, 2018 - Pi Day, also Einstein's birthday. His ashes were interred in Westminster Abbey, between the graves of Isaac Newton and Charles Darwin.
